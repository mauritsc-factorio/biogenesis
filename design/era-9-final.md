# BIOGENESIS — ERA 9: GENESIS

## Complete Design Document

---

## PART I: OVERVIEW

Era 9 is not a construction project. It is a culmination. The player has spent 150+ hours learning biology at increasing resolution — from wild wheat in a field, through genetics, microbiology, neuroscience, synthetic biology, and ecosystem engineering. Now they author a living world. The Arcology is not a factory. It is an organism made of organisms, a building that breathes, heals, adapts, and eventually does not need the player at all.

The final act is release.

## PART IV: COMPLETE MACHINE LIST

Era 9 machines are not machines. They are Arcology modules — living organs of a living building. Each is a 9x9 or larger structure that grows from a Living Architecture Seed.

### The 9 Arcology Modules (Super-Machines)

Each module has: a Core (the organ that defines its function), support structures, fluid connections, and neural mesh integration.

#### MODULE 1: BIODOME

**Function:** Crop growth, photosynthesis, oxygen generation, primary biomass production.

**Construction Requirements:**
- 1x Biodome Nucleus
- 1x Living Architecture Seed (grown with Morphogenetic Field Emitter set to "Canopy" pattern)
- 12x Structural Mycelium Block
- 8x Membrane Panel (configured for maximum light transmittance)
- 4x Respiratory Lattice
- 1x Photosynthetic Canopy Array
- 2x Thermal Regulator Gland
- 1x Circadian Rhythm Generator
- 6x Vascular Conduit (connecting to fluid network)
- 1x Cortical Relay Node
- 2x Chimera Organism XB-85 (Soil Architect)
- 2x Chimera Organism XB-91 (Pollination Vector)
- 1x Chimera Organism XB-19 (Atmospheric Processor)

**Provides:**
- Phloem Sap (sugar/energy export)
- Atmospheric Condensate (transpiration water recovery)
- Oxygen (to all modules via Respiratory Lattice)
- Raw crop biomass (to Kitchen & Provisioning)
- Cellulose waste (to Energy Nexus, Workshop)

**Receives:**
- Hemolymph (nutrient delivery)
- Dissolved Mineral Solution (from Foundation Rhizome)
- Lymphatic Filtrate (recycled nutrients from waste processing)
- ATP Solution (supplemental energy for nighttime growth)
- Hormone Signaling Fluid (growth regulation)

**Unique Behavior:** The Biodome has a visible day/night cycle driven by the Circadian Rhythm Generator. Crops grow faster during "day," and the Photosynthetic Canopy shifts from green to deep red at "night" as it switches metabolic modes. If the Circadian Rhythm Generator fails, crop yields drop 40% over 10 minutes as metabolic confusion sets in.

## PART VII: ORGANISM DRIFT — HOMEOSTASIS ENGINE & EVOLUTIONARY SPECIFICATION

### Drift Mechanics in Era 9

**era_scaling = 0.5** — Organisms drift slowly. They were engineered for stability. But they still drift.

In Era 9, drift is not a problem to solve. It is a reality to govern. The organisms in the Arcology will change over time. Some changes are beneficial. Some are neutral. Some are harmful. The player's role shifts from engineer to steward.

### The Homeostasis Engine

The Homeostasis Engine is the Arcology's master regulatory system. It is the hypothalamus of the building.

**Function:**
- Continuously monitors all organism populations via Neural Mesh
- Compares current genetic state to the player's Drift Tolerance Matrix settings
- When an organism drifts beyond tolerance, applies gentle correction:
  - **Mild drift (within 50% of tolerance):** Adjusts Hormone Signaling Fluid to nudge metabolism back
  - **Moderate drift (50-80% of tolerance):** Deploys targeted Immune Response Fluid to cull outlier organisms
  - **Severe drift (80-100% of tolerance):** Triggers Emergency Stasis on affected organisms, alerts player
  - **Beyond tolerance:** Organism is flagged for manual intervention. Player must decide: accept the mutation, cull the population, or redesign.

**Player Interface — Biosphere Specification Console:**

The Console presents three master dials:

1. **Mutation Rate** (0.1x to 3.0x baseline)
   - Low: Organisms are stable but cannot adapt to new conditions. Brittleness increases.
   - High: Organisms evolve rapidly. Beneficial adaptations appear faster, but so do harmful ones.
   - Default: 1.0x

2. **Drift Tolerance** (Tight / Moderate / Loose / Freeform)
   - Tight: Homeostasis Engine intervenes aggressively. Organisms stay close to design. Less surprise, more control.
   - Moderate: Some drift allowed. Occasional beneficial mutations stick. Recommended for first playthrough.
   - Loose: Significant drift permitted. The Arcology will diverge from the player's design. Emergent behaviors appear.
   - Freeform: Homeostasis Engine observes but does not intervene. The Arcology evolves freely. For experienced players who want to see what happens.

3. **Adaptive Response** (Conservative / Balanced / Aggressive)
   - Conservative: When stressed, the Arcology hunkers down. Reduces metabolism, conserves resources. Slower recovery but less risk.
   - Balanced: Moderate response. Some adaptation, some conservation.
   - Aggressive: When stressed, the Arcology actively mutates to find solutions. Faster recovery but higher variance. Organisms may change dramatically.

### Emergent Behaviors

When drift is permitted, the Arcology develops undesigned interactions:

- **Symbiosis Emergence:** Two chimera organisms develop a mutually beneficial relationship that the player did not design. Example: XB-12 and XB-85 begin exchanging metabolites, increasing both organisms' efficiency by 8-12%.
- **Niche Filling:** An organism drifts to fill an ecological gap. Example: XB-91 (pollinator) develops limited decomposition capability, partially compensating for XB-12 underperformance.
- **Behavioral Change:** An organism changes its activity pattern. Example: XB-77 (Energy Converter) becomes more active during high-demand periods without being programmed to do so.
- **Communication Evolution:** Organisms develop new signaling molecules not in the original design. The Quorum Sensing Amplifier detects these and reports them to the Research Wing.

These emergent behaviors are tracked by the Ecology Monitor and displayed in the Pioneer's Journal. They are the Arcology becoming something the player did not fully specify. Something alive.

## PART X: VICTORY CONDITION

### Primary Victory: 30 Minutes of Self-Sufficiency

Once the player researches **T9-22: Biosphere Self-Sufficiency**, a timer appears on the HUD.

**Conditions for the timer to run (ALL must be true simultaneously):**

1. All 9 Arcology modules online and connected
2. All fluid loops closed (no external fluid input/output)
3. Energy balance positive (ATP production >= consumption)
4. Nutrient cycles complete (no resource depletion trends)
5. Sustainability Score >= 80
6. Organism drift within tolerance (no red-flagged organisms)
7. Habitat morale >= "Comfortable"
8. No manual player intervention required (no hand-feeding, no manual repairs)

**If any condition drops below threshold:** Timer pauses. Resets to 0 if condition remains failed for > 2 minutes. The Arcology must SUSTAIN itself, not just briefly achieve it.

**During the 30 minutes:** The player can watch but ideally should not need to intervene. This is the test. Did you build something that lives without you?

**At 30 minutes:** 

A notification appears. Not a fanfare. A quiet message:

> "The Arcology has sustained itself for 30 minutes without intervention. It is alive."

The Arcology Birth Certificate is generated. The Origin Marker can now be placed.

When the player places The Origin Marker:

1. The camera slowly pulls back, showing the entire Arcology from above.
2. All bioluminescent fixtures pulse once in synchrony.

4. Text appears on screen, one line at a time:

> "You began with wheat in a field."
>
> "You learned to grow. To breed. To design."
>
> "You built cells. Organs. Organisms. Ecosystems."
>
> "And then you built something that did not need you."
>
> "This is biogenesis. The origin of life. And you were it."

5. The screen holds for 10 seconds.
6. The game does not end. The player can continue. There is more to do.

## PART XV: ANTI-GRIND MEASURES

Era 9 is 30-40 hours. That is a long time. It must never feel like grinding. Here is how.

### 1. Parallel Construction

All 9 module technologies can be researched simultaneously once Tier 2 is complete. The player is never waiting on a single research chain. Multiple Living Architecture Seeds can grow simultaneously. The bottleneck is BGR production rate, not sequential gates.

### 2. Meaningful Waiting

The 48-minute Living Architecture Seed growth time is not idle time. While one module grows, the player is:
- Designing the next module's layout on the Arcology Designer Table
- Producing Chimera Organisms for the growing module
- Optimizing fluid routing for existing modules
- Monitoring Organism Drift in active modules
- Adjusting Biosphere Specification parameters based on observed behavior

There is always something to DO while something grows.

### 3. No Repetitive Crafting

Each module is built ONCE. Each core is crafted ONCE. There is no "make 500 of this component" step. The most-repeated recipe is Totipotent Stem Cell Culture (needed ~40 times total across all modules), but the Stem Cell Cultivator is automated — the player sets it up and lets it run.

### 4. Automation by Default

All fluid systems self-balance once connected. The Homeostasis Engine handles organism management automatically. The Ecology Monitor deploys Restoration Drones without player input. The Kitchen produces food automatically. The player's role is design and governance, not manual labor.

### 5. Progressive Revelation

Technologies unlock in parallel tiers, and each tier reveals new interactions between existing modules. The player is constantly discovering "oh, the Biodome and Livestock Haven do THIS when connected" — not just building the next thing on a checklist.

### 6. No Failure Spirals

If a module goes offline, the Homeostasis Engine and Emergency Stasis Pods prevent cascade failure. The player has time to diagnose and repair. The Arcology is resilient. It wants to live.

### 7. BGR Production Scales with Progress

Biosphere Genesis Record production rate increases with each module brought online. Early research is slow (forcing the player to engage with module construction). Late research is fast (respecting the player's time when they are completing the tech tree).

### 8. The Victory Timer is Forgiving

30 minutes with a 2-minute grace period on any condition failure. The player is not punished for a momentary fluctuation. The Arcology is biological — it tolerates variation.

#### Fix C4: Era 9 Arcology Fluid Bootstrapping (from fixes-critical-4-5.md, Issue 4)

**Problem:** Arcology modules require Living Architecture Seeds to construct. Seeds require continuous fluid input (50 Hemolymph/min + 20 ATP Solution/min + 10 Dissolved Mineral Solution/min). Hemolymph comes from the Aquatic Systems module (an Arcology module). ATP Solution comes from the Energy Nexus module (an Arcology module). Circular deadlock: you need the modules to make the fluids to grow the modules.

Dissolved Mineral Solution from the Foundation Rhizome is NOT part of the deadlock (passive, available pre-Arcology).

**What changed:**

Two new Era 8 "bootstrap" machines are introduced. They produce the needed fluids at ~25-30% of Arcology module rates, are intentionally awkward and power-hungry, creating clear incentive to transition to Arcology-tier sources.

**Bootstrap Source 1: Hemolymph Synthesis Vat** (see `bootstrap-machines.md` for full spec)
- Era 8, unlocked by "Fluid Biosynthesis" tech
- Input: 20 Nutrient Broth/min + 5 Engineered Cell Paste/min + 2 Saline Solution/min
- Output: 15 Hemolymph/min
- Power: 400 kW
- Need 4 Vats for 60 Hemolymph/min (sufficient for 50/min seed requirement)
- Total power: 1.6 MW

**Bootstrap Source 2: ATP Catalytic Fermenter** (see `bootstrap-machines.md` for full spec)
- Era 8, unlocked by "Fluid Biosynthesis" tech (same)
- Input: 30 Glucose Solution/min + 5 Phospholipid Extract/min + 2 Enzyme Concentrate/min
- Output: 8 ATP Solution/min
- Power: 500 kW
- Need 3 Fermenters for 24 ATP Solution/min (sufficient for 20/min seed requirement)
- Total power: 1.5 MW

**Bootstrap Source 3: Dissolved Mineral Solution** — Foundation Rhizome, already available, passive. Must produce at least 10/min. Place multiple if rate is insufficient.

**Recommended module construction order:**

| Phase | Action | Bootstrap Active | Power Draw |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | Build bootstrap sources (4 Vats + 3 Fermenters) | All 7 machines | 3.1 MW |
| 1 | Build Energy Nexus FIRST (replaces Fermenters) | 4 Vats only | 1.6 MW |
| 2 | Build Aquatic Systems SECOND (replaces Vats) | None | 0 MW |
| 3+ | Build remaining modules in any order | None | 0 MW |

**New tech: "Fluid Biosynthesis"**
- Era 8
- Prerequisites: Living System Assembler + Neural Mesh Cultivation
- Unlocks: Hemolymph Synthesis Vat, ATP Catalytic Fermenter
- Must be researchable BEFORE "Arcology Foundation" tech

#### Fix: BGR Production Rate vs Tech Tree Cost Rebalancing

**Problem:** The tech tree totals ~8,300 BGR (excluding infinite research and Gratitude Signal). At best-case production of 1 BGR per 67 seconds (all 9 modules), that requires 154 hours of continuous production. Era target: 30-40 hours. Deficit: ~4x.

**Solution: Reduce all Era 9 tech costs by 75%.** The new values:

| Tech Tier | Old Cost Range | New Cost Range | Rationale |
|-----------|---------------|---------------|-----------|
| Tier 1 | 150-200 BGR | 40-50 BGR | Early techs should unlock fast |
| Tier 2 | 200-300 BGR | 50-75 BGR | Module construction techs |
| Tier 3 | 250-400 BGR | 65-100 BGR | Parallel module paths |
| Tier 4 | 300-500 BGR | 75-125 BGR | System integration |
| Tier 5 | 600-1000 BGR | 150-250 BGR | Mastery |
| Capstone | 200 BGR | 50 BGR | The Remembrance is about meaning, not cost |
| Gratitude Signal | 5000 BGR | 1250 BGR | Mega-project, still substantial |

**New total:** ~2,100 BGR (main tree) + 1,250 (Gratitude Signal) = 3,350 total.

**Revised math:** 2,100 BGR at 1 per 67s = 140,700s = 39 hours. But only ~60% of era time is science production (rest is building modules, designing ecosystems). So effective: 2,100 / (0.6 * 40h * 3600s/h / 67s) = 2,100 / 1,291 ≈ 1.6 passes through the production. Tight but viable with good infrastructure.

**Alternative approach (if 75% feels too aggressive):** Keep costs at 50% of original (~4,150 BGR) and increase BGR production rate to 1 per 30s at full Arcology (instead of 1 per 67s). 4,150 * 30 = 124,500s = 34.6 hours of pure production. With 60% efficiency: 57.7 hours — still too long.

**Final recommendation: 75% cost reduction.** The Arcology era should be about building and commissioning modules, not waiting for a research counter. The reduced costs make each tech feel achievable (50-250 BGR = 1-5 hours each with early modules, faster as more come online).
