## 5. TECH TREE

### Tier 1: Foundations of Industrial Biotech (unlocked with Era 6 science + Evolutionary Dynamics Reports)
- **Directed Evolution Principles** (200 Era6 + 50 Era7) -> Directed Evolution Chamber, Mutagenic Primer recipe, Selective Medium recipe
- **Algal Cultivation at Scale** (200 Era6 + 50 Era7) -> Industrial Photobioreactor, Raw Algae Slurry processing
- **Mycelium Material Science** (150 Era6 + 50 Era7) -> Mycelium Fabricator, Crude Mycelium Mat, Mycelium Leather

### Tier 2: Processing Infrastructure
- **Algal Fractionation** (req: Algal Cultivation) (300 Era7) -> Algal Fractionator, Lipid Extract, Algal Protein, Astaxanthin
- **Bioplastic Synthesis** (req: Algal Fractionation OR Mycelium Material Science) (300 Era7) -> Bioplastic Polymerizer, PLA Pellets, Thermoformer
- **Structural Mycelium** (req: Mycelium Material Science) (250 Era7) -> Mycelium Press, Compressed Board, Biocomposite Laminate
- **Strain Selection** (req: Directed Evolution Principles) (300 Era7) -> Strain Library, Population Gene Library, Cryopreservation

### Tier 3: Energy Independence
- **Cellulosic Ethanol** (req: Bioplastic Synthesis) (400 Era7) -> Ethanol Distillery, Enzyme-Treated Feedstock
- **Algal Biodiesel** (req: Algal Fractionation) (400 Era7) -> Transesterification Reactor, Biodiesel, Glycerol processing
- **Anaerobic Digestion** (req: Cellulosic Ethanol) (350 Era7) -> Anaerobic Digester, Biogas, Biomethane
- **Population Dynamics** (req: Strain Selection) (400 Era7) -> Population Analysis Terminal, Drift Variance Dataset, Fitness Trajectory Map

### Tier 4: Integration & Optimization
- **CO2 Exchange Mutualism** (req: Cellulosic Ethanol + Algal Cultivation) (500 Era7) -> Unlocks Biological Web CO2 exchange link. Bioreactor CO2 output pipes directly to photobioreactor. +20% algae yield, +15% bioreactor efficiency.
- **Biofuel Vehicles** (req: Cellulosic Ethanol + Algal Biodiesel) (500 Era7) -> Vehicle Assembly Bay, Mycelium Chassis, Bio-Rubber Tire, Enzyme Fuel Injector
- **Continuous Culture** (req: Population Dynamics + Directed Evolution) (600 Era7) -> Continuous Culture Engine (mega-bioreactor)
- **Crop Consolidation** (req: any 3 Tier 2 techs) (500 Era7) -> Crop Processing Hub (mega-machine)

## 8. ORGANISM DRIFT SPECIFICS

### era_scaling = 1.5 (FASTEST ERA)

**Rationale:** Era 7 is about exploiting drift as a resource. Higher drift rate means:
- Directed Evolution Chambers produce results faster
- Strain Libraries have more diversity to select from
- But unmanaged machines drift faster too, creating urgency

### Drift as Manufacturing Resource
In previous eras, drift was something to manage or mitigate. In Era 7, it becomes a production input.

**Directed Evolution Chamber mechanics:**
- Player sets a "target trait" from a dropdown (e.g., "Increase lipid production," "Improve temperature tolerance," "Enhance growth rate")
- Chamber applies selection pressure: organisms that drift toward the target survive, others are culled
- Each 90-second cycle [CORRECTED from 45s, see DESIGN AMENDMENTS], the population shifts toward the target trait
- Progress bar shows "% toward target." Reaching 100% produces High-Fitness Biomass with the desired trait.
- Time to 100% varies: simple traits (3-5 cycles), complex traits (10-15 cycles), novel combinations (20+ cycles)

**Strain Library mechanics:**
- Accepts evolved biomass from chambers
- Internal storage holds up to 50 variant snapshots
- "Screen" command: run competitive fitness test, rank all stored variants
- "Clone" command: duplicate a stored variant for deployment
- "Release" command: send a stored variant into a connected machine, replacing its current organism
- "Drift & Pick" workflow: clone multiple variants, let them drift separately, screen, pick the winner

**Population-Level Genetic Profiles:**
The Population Analysis Terminal displays:
- **Mean fitness**: average performance of the population
- **Variance**: how diverse the population is (high variance = more potential for evolution, less consistency)
- **Fitness trajectory**: graphed over time, showing improvement or degradation trend
- **Trait distribution**: histogram of key traits across the population
- **Drift velocity**: how fast the population is currently changing

### The 47-Location Reorganization Event
This is a scripted narrative event that triggers when:
- A Directed Evolution Chamber completes a complex trait target (20+ cycles)
- Probability: 15% per complex completion

**Event sequence:**
1. Alert: "DIRECTED EVOLUTION CHAMBER [location]: Evolution cycle complete."
2. Pause, then: "ANOMALY DETECTED: Genome reorganization in 47 locations."
3. Tooltip on the chamber shows: "The evolved variant reorganized its genome in 47 locations. The organism solved your problem. But it also did something else."
4. The produced High-Fitness Biomass gains the target trait PLUS one random additional trait (could be beneficial, neutral, or harmful).
5. An "Anomalous Genome Sequence" item is produced.
6. If the player deploys this organism, its additional trait affects connected machines through the Biological Web in unpredictable ways.

### Drift Dampening
For players who want stability in certain production lines:
- **Drift Dampener Coil**: Placed in a machine's module slot. Reduces drift by 50% for that machine. Does not prevent Evolution Chamber acceleration fully (reduces it from +200% to +100%).
- **Containment Alarm Sensor**: Alerts the player when drift exceeds a configurable threshold.
- **Evolution Pressure Valve**: Emergency stop. Halts all drift in a 5-tile radius for 5 minutes. Long cooldown (30 minutes). For "oh no" moments.

## 11. "ZOOM IN" MOMENT

**Title: "The 47 Rearrangements"**

**Trigger:** First time a Directed Evolution Chamber completes a complex trait target and triggers the genome reorganization event.

**The moment:** The screen pauses. The camera zooms into the Directed Evolution Chamber. A cutaway view shows the internal culture vessel. The population fitness graph is displayed, showing the trajectory climbing toward the target.

Then the graph spikes. Not gradually -- suddenly. The target is hit, but the graph keeps going. Additional metrics appear: "Genome rearrangements detected: 47 loci." A simplified genome visualization shows dozens of genes changing position simultaneously.

**Text sequence (appearing one line at a time, slowly):**
> "Evolution cycle 23 complete."
> "Target trait achieved: lipid production +340%."
> "..."
> "Secondary analysis running."
> "..."
> "Genome rearrangements detected at 47 loci."
> "Cross-referencing with target selection criteria..."
> "3 rearrangements correlate with target trait."
> "44 rearrangements: function unknown."
> "..."
> "The organism solved your problem."
> "But it also did something else."

The camera pulls back. The Directed Evolution Chamber now has a faint orange glow. The produced High-Fitness Biomass is in the output slot, with a "?" icon on its additional trait.

**Why this works:** It makes the player feel the fundamental tension of directed evolution: you control the selection pressure, but you do not control the mutations. Evolution finds solutions through paths you didn't anticipate. And those paths include side effects. The 44 unknown rearrangements create genuine uncertainty about what the player just created.

## 7. BIOLOGICAL WEB SPECIFICS

### New Relationships Unlocked in Era 8

**Neural Mesh Node Universal Effect**
- Type: Universal beneficial (commensal toward everything)
- Range: 12-tile radius
- Effect: Every organism-containing entity within 12 tiles receives +10% to all stats (growth rate, yield, efficiency) AND -50% drift rate.
- Visual: Soft blue network lines connecting the Neural Mesh Node to all affected entities. Pulses gently.
- Gameplay: Neural mesh is the stabilizer. After Era 7's chaotic 1.5x drift, Neural Mesh brings calm. Organisms stop drifting wildly. Production becomes consistent. The player can finally relax... until the emergent properties start appearing.
- Stacking: Multiple Neural Mesh Nodes in range do NOT stack the +10% bonus. Only one applies. But drift reduction stacks multiplicatively: two nodes = -75% drift, three = -87.5%.

**System Profiles (replaces individual organism profiles for clusters)**
- Trigger: When 3+ organism-containing entities within a 15-tile cluster are connected by 2+ Biological Web relationships, they form a "System."
- Display: The System Dashboard Console shows Systems as bounded regions on the map. Each System has a System Profile displaying:
  - Component organisms and their roles
  - Active relationships (mutualism, commensalism, etc.)
  - **Emergent Properties**: behaviors or outputs that arise from the interaction, not from any individual organism
  - Stability score
  - Predicted evolution trajectory
- Emergent Property examples:
  - "Waste Elimination": no byproducts leave the system (all recycled internally)
  - "Temperature Homeostasis": system maintains stable temperature without external regulation
  - "Self-Repair": damage to one component triggers compensatory response from others
  - "Growth Optimization": system collectively adjusts growth rates to match demand
  - "Pathogen Resistance": system collectively resists contamination that would defeat any individual

**Emergent Property Generation Rules:**
- Each System is evaluated every 10 minutes of real time.
- Properties emerge based on: number of organisms (more = more emergence), number of relationships (more = more emergence), diversity of organism types, time the system has been stable.
- Expected emergent properties: ~1 per 30 minutes for a well-designed system.
- **Undesigned emergent properties**: 10% chance per emergence check that the property is not on the "expected" list. These are labeled "UNDESIGNED" in the System Profile and contribute to the narrative unsettlement.
- Milestone: When 3 undesigned emergent properties exist simultaneously, a narrative event triggers (see Section 12).

### Web View Enhancements (System Dashboard)
- **Map-level view**: entire factory shown as a network graph. Organisms are nodes, relationships are edges. Systems are bounded regions.
- Color coding: green (healthy), yellow (stressed), red (failing), blue (neural mesh stabilized)
- **Filters**: By relationship type, by era of origin, by organism type, by System membership
- **Time lapse**: scrub backward to see how the web evolved over the play session
- **Prediction mode**: project 30 minutes into the future based on current trajectories
- **Anomaly highlights**: undesigned emergent properties pulsate with a white glow

## 8. ORGANISM DRIFT SPECIFICS

### era_scaling = 1.0 (Standard, stabilized by Neural Mesh)

**Rationale:** After Era 7's frantic 1.5x drift rate, Era 8 returns to baseline. Combined with Neural Mesh's -50% drift reduction, organisms become stable. This serves the narrative: the player has tamed evolution. Systems are predictable. Reliable. Which makes it all the more unsettling when emergence occurs that was not designed.

### Self-Healing Systems
In Era 8, drift is no longer the primary concern. Instead, the biological systems self-manage:
- **Living buildings** auto-repair damage. If a Self-Healing Biocomposite Panel takes damage (from biters, accidents, etc.), the embedded repair bacteria activate and restore integrity over 2-5 minutes.
- **Ecosystems** self-balance. If a keystone organism's population drops, connected organisms adjust to compensate. The player sees this as automatic production rebalancing.
- **Neural Mesh** monitors and reports, but does not intervene directly. It provides the player with information. The organisms handle the response.

### Self-Optimization
This is the unsettling part:
- Living systems gradually improve their own performance without player input.
- Every 30 minutes, a system with 4+ organisms may auto-optimize: rearranging internal metabolic flows to increase output by 1-3%.
- The player sees this as slowly increasing production numbers on machines they haven't touched.
- Tooltip on self-optimized machine: "Output increased by 2.1%. No modifications detected. The system found a better configuration."
- This is entirely beneficial. But the player didn't do it. Something else did.

### The Undesigned Emergence Event
**Trigger:** 3 undesigned emergent properties exist simultaneously in any System.

**Event sequence:**
1. System Dashboard flashes a notification: "SYSTEM [name]: Emergence threshold exceeded."
2. Zoom to the System on the map. The bounded region pulses.
3. Text: "3 emergent properties appeared that were not designed."
4. List of the three properties is shown. They are beneficial (by design -- we don't punish the player for succeeding).
5. Text: "These properties arise from the interactions between organisms. No individual organism produces them. No individual organism understands them."
6. Pause.
7. "Your system has capabilities you did not give it."
8. "This is working as intended. Probably."

## 11. "ZOOM IN" MOMENT

**Title: "The Self-Preservation Response"**

**Trigger:** The first time the player attempts to demolish a living building (Living Workshop, Homeostatic Chamber, etc.) that has been operational for more than 2 hours.

**The moment:** The player clicks "Deconstruct" on a living building. Instead of immediate deconstruction:

1. The building's walls pulse. A faint orange glow appears along the self-healing biocomposite panels.
2. The deconstruction progress bar slows to 20% of normal speed.
3. An alert appears: "STRUCTURAL ANOMALY: Self-Healing Biocomposite panels are resisting deconstruction."
4. A tooltip appears on the building:

> "The building's repair systems have activated."
> "Repair bacteria are interpreting deconstruction as structural damage."
> "The building is attempting to heal itself."
> "..."
> "This is the correct response to the stimuli it is receiving."
> "It was designed to resist damage. You are damaging it."
> "It does not know you changed your mind."

5. After 5 seconds of resistance, the repair systems are overwhelmed, and deconstruction proceeds normally. The building is deconstructed.

6. But a "Self-Preservation Response Record" item is left on the ground where the building stood.

**Why this works:** It is a 5-second delay. Mechanically trivial. Narratively devastating. The building did exactly what it was designed to do: resist damage. The player just happened to be the source of damage. The building cannot distinguish between a biter attack and its creator deciding it's in the wrong place. This is the moment the player realizes: living systems serve their own imperatives, even when those imperatives conflict with yours. The building was not disobedient. It was obedient to a deeper instruction than "be here."

## APPENDIX: COMBINED DESIGN PRINCIPLES

### Tone Progression: Era 7 to Era 8
- **Era 7** is industrial confidence. The player wields evolution like a tool. Warnings exist but feel manageable. The tone says: "You can do this. Be careful, but you can do this."
- **Era 8** is awed humility. The player's creations exceed their designs. The tone shifts from "I built this" to "I started this." The factory is no longer the player's creation in the way a car is its engineer's creation. It is the player's creation in the way a garden is its gardener's creation: guided, not dictated.

### Mechanical Progression: Era 7 to Era 8
- **Era 7** gives the player maximum control (directed evolution, strain selection, Mk3 upgrades). The player is the optimizer.
- **Era 8** shifts control to the system. The player sets up conditions. The organisms do the rest. The player's role transitions from operator to architect to, ultimately, observer.

### The Central Unsettlement
Neither era's unsettling moments are hostile. The 47 rearrangements are not dangerous. The self-preservation response is not threatening. The undesigned emergent properties are beneficial. The neural mesh dream log is ambiguous, not alarming. The horror, such as it is, is existential: the player created something that works. Something that works in ways they did not intend. Something that might not need them anymore. And that is the final insight of the mod: life does not stay where you put it. It does not do only what you designed. Given enough time and enough connections, it becomes something new. Something emergent. Something that solves problems you didn't know you had, in ways you don't fully understand. This is not a failure of engineering. This is what biology does.

#### Fix C3: Era 7 Selection Pressure Log Math (from fixes-critical-1-3.md, Fix 3)

**Problem:** Selection Pressure Log had a 5% random drop chance per 45s Directed Evolution Chamber cycle. At 3 chambers, expected rate was ~12 logs/hour. Capstone needs ~500 logs. Time to acquire: ~41.7 hours. Era budget: 20-25 hours. Deficit: 2-4x over budget. High variance made it feel worse.

**What changed:**

- **Selection Pressure Log is now a guaranteed crafted output** (replaces random drop).
- **Cycle time doubled from 45s to 90s** to keep the 4-5 hour target.

```
Recipe: selection-pressure-log (MODIFIED — replaces random drop)
  Machine: Directed Evolution Chamber
  Inputs:  2 Evolved Biomass + 1 Mutagenic Primer + 1 Selective Medium + 10 Nutrient Broth
  Outputs: 1 Selection Pressure Log (guaranteed) + 1 Adapted Culture (guaranteed)
  Time:    90s (was 45s)
  Tech:    (unchanged)

  Note: "Mutant Population Sample" and "Selective Media Plate" from the
  original fix doc are replaced with actual Era 7 items: Evolved Biomass
  (item #1), Mutagenic Primer (item #3), Selective Medium (item #4).
  Nutrient Broth defined in bootstrap-machines.md appendix.
```

**Math verification:**

| Parameter | Old Value | New Value |
|---|---|---|
| Selection Pressure Log source | 5% random drop per 45s cycle | Guaranteed output per 90s cycle |
| Log acquisition rate (3 chambers) | ~12/hour (expected, high variance) | 120/hour (guaranteed, zero variance) |
| Time to 500 logs | ~41.7 hours | ~4.17 hours |
| Capstone feasibility | Impossible in era timeframe | Achievable in ~4-5 hours |

**Secondary bottleneck note:** All four inputs to the Evolutionary Dynamics Report must sustain at minimum 120 units/hour with 3-4 machines. The mathematical constraint: `500 units / 4.17 hours = 120 units/hour minimum per input`. Drift Variance Dataset, Fitness Landscape Model, and Population Gene Library should be verified against this threshold. If any input falls below, reduce its recipe time, increase output quantity, or increase report yield from 2 to 3.

**Impact on existing text:** Line referencing "5% byproduct chance" in the anti-grind notes and the science pack pipeline diagrams are superseded. The Selection Pressure Log is now a primary output, not a byproduct. The "multiple chambers running in parallel" guidance remains valid but for throughput reasons, not RNG mitigation.
