# BIOGENESIS: COMPLETE PLAYTHROUGH SIMULATION

## Total projected playtime: ~165-215 hours

---

## ERA 1: OBSERVATION (target: 1-1.5h)

### HOUR 0:00-0:30: Landfall and Survival

The player crashes on the planet. They are hungry.

- **Minutes 0-5:** The player moves through the grassland biome, collects Wild Grass (fiber source), Wild Grain Head, and Clover Patch. The foraging mechanic is mobile; all four biomes require movement. This feels like exploration, not mining.
- **Minutes 5-15:** The player crafts a Crude Hammer (2 Stone + 1 Stick) and a Knapping Blade (1 Stone + 1 Stick). They process Wild Grass into Plant Fiber, twist it into Fiber Cord. They move to the Forest Biome and gather Forest Nut Cluster and Bracket Fungus. To the Wetland for Cattail and Bog Root. Peat Moss collected for fuel.
- **Minutes 15-25:** The player builds a Grinding Slab (1 Flat Stone + 4 Stone) and Pestle, mills Wild Grain Heads into Rough Flour. They build a Stone-Lined Fire Pit (8 Stone + 4 Fiber Cord + 2 Peat Brick) and cook Flatbread (Rough Flour + Water). First food is secured. They set up a Leaching Basket near water for nuts, a Drying Rack for fungus. Both are passive -- player walks away and forages.
- **Minutes 25-30:** Seeds begin appearing as byproducts: Wild Grass Seed (~20% from grass processing), Wild Grain Seed (~15% from threshing chaff). The player has been finding them without specifically trying. The Field Notebook unlocks "Forager's Eye" (forage 20 plants) and "Material Properties" (process 10 items).

**Emotional state:** Curious, active, engaged. The player is moving constantly between biomes. No standing still. Excitement: 7/10.

### HOUR 0:30-1:00: First Automation and Awakening

- **Minutes 30-35:** The player builds the Water Wheel (8 Wood + 4 Fiber Cord + 2 Stone) on a river tile and connects it to a Grinding Slab via a Fiber Cord belt. **This is the first automation moment.** Grain milling runs without the player. They drop Wild Grain Heads into an input basket; flour appears in a Collection Basket. The player is free to explore while the machine works.
- **Minutes 35-45:** The player loads the Compost Heap (5 Green Waste + 5 Brown Waste) -- 4-minute passive cycle. They explore all four biomes collecting specimens. At ~3% drop rate, they may find a Strange Bioluminescent Fungus or an Ancient Seed Pod. If they find the Anomalous Grass Specimen (~5%), the tooltip reads: "This one is... different. Bigger. Healthier." Filed away subconsciously.
- **Minutes 45-55:** The player discovers their third seed type (Cattail Seed from stalk processing, Bog Root Tuber Eye from pounding). Research "Seeds of Potential" unlocks. The Specimen Log UI appears: each seed type shows "Origin: Wild. Traits: Unknown." **This is the Zoom In moment.** The game formally acknowledges seeds are individuals with hidden properties.
- **Minutes 55-70:** The player collects the 4th and 5th seed types (Forest Nut Seed from leaching, Wild Grass Seed already found). Composting cycle completes. "Cultivation Hypothesis" research becomes available. The player has all prerequisites: 5 seed types, Compost Heap cycled, Water Wheel operational. They craft ~15-20 Observational Analysis Kits (1 Rough Flour + 1 Dried Fungus + 1 Fiber Cord + 1 Finished Compost = 2 Kits). Research completes.

**Emotional state:** Satisfaction and wonder. The seed discovery reframe was earned, not taught. Excitement: 8/10.

### TRANSITION: Era 1 to Era 2

- **Requirements met:** All 5 seed types (Y), Compost Heap built and cycled (Y), Water Wheel operational (Y).
- **Reward:** Cultivation Starter Kit -- 5x each seed type, Tilled Soil Plot recipe, Simple Planter schematic, Specimen Log upgrade showing "Quality: Wild."
- **Emotional payoff:** The Field Notebook reads: "I have seeds. I have enriched soil. I have water... I can plant." The transition feels earned because the player discovered seeds organically through processing chains they were already running.

### KEY METRICS AT ERA END:
- Items crafted: ~80-120
- Machines built: 7 (Grinding Slab, Drying Rack, Leaching Basket, Fire Pit, Compost Heap, Water Wheel, Collection Basket)
- Science packs produced: ~15-20 Observational Analysis Kits
- Power consumption: Mechanical only (Water Wheel)
- Bio Web entities: ~8-12 (wild plants + Compost Heap, all hidden)
- Organism drift events observed: 0 (too slow at era_scaling 0.3)
- Breadcrumbs found: Fermented Fruit Pulp (100%), Ash (100%), Tannin Water (100%); possibly Bioluminescent Fungus, Ancient Seed Pod, Anomalous Grass Specimen

**COLD START VIABILITY: PASS.** The player begins with access to 4 biomes of forageable plants, stone, wood, and water. No prerequisites missing.

## ERA 2A: GRAIN FARMING (target: 6-8h)

### HOUR 1-2: First Farm

- The player places Tilled Soil Plots using Finished Compost, plants Wheat and Barley from their starter kit seeds. They build a Thresher mk1 (8 Wood + 4 Stone + 4 Fiber Cord + 2 Wooden Gear). Research "Tilled Earth" (2 CED) and "Grain Cultivation" (3 CED) are the first targets.
- **Science pack production issue:** The Controlled Environment Dataset requires Soil Sample Jar + Crop Yield Record + Seed Comparison Slide + Growth Condition Log. The player needs Paper (from Cattail Fiber + Water at a Pulp Press) and Plant-Based Ink (from Beet Juice + Tannin Water). But Beets are not yet available! The review documents flag this as requiring a fix: "Charcoal Ink" as an alternative using Ash (from Era 1). **Assuming the fix is implemented**, the player produces Paper + Charcoal Ink from Era 1 carryovers and begins generating Crop Yield Records at first harvest.
- First wheat harvest arrives. The player processes wheat through Thresher -> Cleaner -> Millstone (Water Wheel powered). Wheat Flour produced. Without yeast yet, bread is not possible -- but the Barley Flatbread fix provides an intermediate food: Barley Flour + Water + Salt = Barley Flatbread. This solves the food gate.
- The player builds Potato plots (from Bog Root Tuber Eye, now Potato Tuber) and begins Root Cultivation research.

**Emotional state:** Focused, methodical. Building a real farm for the first time. Excitement: 6/10.

### HOUR 2-4: Expanding Crops and First Science

- The player unlocks Water-Powered Milling (Millstone mk1 runs from Water Wheel), Basic Baking, and starts the Simple Planter. The Planter automates seed placement -- a significant anti-grind measure.
- Bean Seed discovered on field edges. Legume Cultivation unlocked. Beans planted next to Wheat (the companion planting relationship gives +15% wheat yield, though the player cannot see why yet).
- Science pack production chain established: Soil Sample Jar from farm plots + Crop Yield Records from harvests + Seed Comparison Slides from Specimen Archive + Growth Condition Logs from Farm Plot Markers. Production rate: ~2-3 CED per 10 minutes once running.
- Research progresses through Fiber Retting, Oilseed Cultivation, Herb Cultivation.

**Science pack flow check: PASS.** First science pack achievable within 60-90 minutes of Era 2A start, given Charcoal Ink fix for Paper/Ink.

### HOUR 4-5: "Field Observation" Tech and Crop Vigor Pips

- At approximately tech #5 in Era 2A, the player unlocks "Field Observation." This enables **Crop Vigor Pips** -- small 8x8 pixel green/yellow/red indicators on crop plots. For the first time, the player can see that something spatial is happening. Green pip near compost, red pip in monoculture areas. The player starts experimenting with layout.
- This is a critical design moment. Without the pips, players built suboptimal layouts for 15+ hours and had to tear down later. With pips, they develop correct spatial intuitions early.

**Bio Web first notice: Hour 4-5 of Era 2A.** The player sees pips but does not know the underlying system. They think "crops near compost are green" and "two wheat fields next to each other are red."

**Emotional state:** Growing satisfaction. The farm is taking shape and the pips add a puzzle layer. Excitement: 7/10.

### HOUR 5-8: Full Era 2A

- The player establishes 6+ crop types, builds the textile chain (Retting Pool -> Flax Breaker -> Scutching Board -> Spinning Wheel -> Loom). Retting takes 105s (two 60s stages after timer fix), not 180s.
- Seed Sorter mk1 unlocked. The player begins selecting Farm-quality seeds from harvests. First engagement with the selection mechanic. The Genetic Profile appears on crops: "Yield: ???, Growth Rate: ???, Hardiness: ???."
- Mechanical Harvester built -- 5x5 area automated harvesting. The player designs larger field layouts.
- By hour 7-8, the player has ~18 technologies researched, ~60 items encountered, and has unlocked yeast cultivation from the barley chain.

### TRANSITION: Era 2A to Era 2B

- **Trigger:** Completing the yeast cultivation technology.
- **Reward:** Starter Culture Kit -- 10x Yeast Culture for free. This eliminates the barley -> malt -> mash -> wort -> ferment chain cold-start (which would otherwise require ~30 minutes of passive waiting).
- **Emotional payoff:** The narrative frame ("a neighboring settlement shares their starter culture") is thin but mechanically generous. The player immediately has yeast for bread.

## ERA 2B: APPLIED AGRICULTURE (target: 6-8h)

### HOUR 8-10: Bread, Beer, and Fermentation

- With the Starter Culture Kit's 10x Yeast Culture, the player immediately bakes Bread Loaf (Wheat Flour + Yeast Culture + Water). First bread. This is satisfying -- the wheat chain that started in Era 1 pays off.
- The player builds the full barley fermentation chain (Malting Floor -> Mash Tun -> Brew Kettle -> Fermenter) to produce their own yeast sustainably. Beer appears as a byproduct and trade good.
- Sauerkraut production: Cabbage + Salt (lacto-fermented, no yeast needed). This correctly teaches a different fermentation mechanism.
- Preservation Science unlocked: Vinegar (from oxidized beer), Brine, Pickling.

**Emotional state:** Productive. Many chains are converging. Excitement: 6/10.

### HOUR 10-14: The Soil and Selection Deep Dive

- Soil management becomes serious: Enriched Compost (Finished Compost + Ash + Clover Mulch), Soil Amendments (Nitrogen, Potassium, Calcium). Soil tiers rise from 1 to 3 to 4. Soil Mixer mk1 for the final tier.
- Seed Sorter mk2 unlocked. Select-quality seeds produced (+20-30% improvement). The player sees consistent outperformance from Select seeds and attributes it to selection -- correctly.
- Beehive placement, Worm Farm built. The Bio Web effects are now strongly felt: crops near Beehive + Companion Flowers yield 25% more. Wheat near beans yields 15% more. The player is designing farm layouts deliberately based on pip feedback.
- Specimen Archive built and placed near farm plots. Organism snapshots stored for the first time.

### HOUR 14-16: The Variation Hypothesis

- "Variation Hypothesis" research (tech #40) requires Specimen Archive + Advanced Seed Selection + 30 other technologies. When it completes, the Genetic Profile partially unlocks: one trait per crop becomes visible. A wheat crop might show "Yield: 147% [Wild Baseline: 100%]." **This is the Era 2 Zoom In moment.** The player realizes they have been performing genetic selection for 14+ hours.
- "Cultivation Mastery" (tech #41) is the era transition research. It requires: 50 total CED spent, Enriched Compost produced, Seed Sorter mk2 built.

### TRANSITION: Era 2 to Era 3

- **Requirements met:** All mk2 upgrades researched (Y), Variation Hypothesis completed (Y), Cultivation Mastery researched (Y).
- **Reward:** Access to "The Invisible Ecosystem" research chapter.
- **Emotional payoff:** The player has transformed from forager to systematic farmer-scientist. The partially visible Genetic Profile is a hook for what comes next.

### KEY METRICS AT ERA END:
- Items crafted: ~800-1200
- Machines built: ~29 (mk1 and mk2 tiers)
- Science packs produced: ~185 CED
- Power consumption: Mechanical (Water Wheels, multiple)
- Bio Web entities: ~40-60 (all crops, compost bins, beehives, worm farms, irrigation)
- Organism drift events observed: ~84-108 per organism (mostly unnoticed; attentive players see yield variance)
- Breadcrumbs found: Mutant Crop Specimen (~2%), Soil Worm with Unusual Coloration (~3%), possibly Crystalline Beet Sugar (~1%)

**POWER AVAILABILITY: PASS.** Era 2 runs on Water Wheels (no kW grid needed). Demand is ~10-50 kW equivalent; 2 Bio-Fiber Water Turbines provide 60 kW.

## ERA 3: THE INVISIBLE ECOSYSTEM (target: 14-18h)

### HOUR 1-3: Animals and the Microscopic Revolution

- The player researches Animal Husbandry I (150 MCD) and Fermentation Basics (200 MCD). MCD production chain: Fermentation Observation Log + Soil Microbiology Report + Colony Morphology Plate + Microbial Population Count = 2 MCD at the Microbial Analysis Bench (20s).
- **Science pack cold start check:** The MCD recipe requires a Fermentation Observation Log (from running fermentations, which the player brought from Era 2B) and a Soil Microbiology Report (from Soil Composition Analyzer, which requires T-3.17 Soil Microbiology to unlock). But T-3.17 costs 250 MCD. **Potential issue:** The player needs MCD to research the tech that enables MCD component production.
- **Resolution:** The tech tree shows T-3.12 (Fermentation Basics) and T-3.01 (Animal Husbandry I) as the entry techs, both costing MCD. But T-3.17 requires T-3.12 as a prerequisite, and the Microbial Analysis Bench requires T-3.24. This means there must be an earlier, simpler science pack recipe or a bootstrap mechanism. Looking at the tech tree, T-3.12 requires MCD but can be the first tech researched -- so MCD must be producible without the Soil Composition Analyzer. The recipe requires: Fermentation Observation Log (from running a Fermentation Vat, available at T-3.12 unlock), Soil Microbiology Report (requires Soil Composition Analyzer), Colony Morphology Plate (requires Clean Bench, T-3.18), and Microbial Population Count (requires Counting Chamber, T-3.24).

**CRITICAL FINDING: CIRCULAR DEPENDENCY IN ERA 3 SCIENCE PACK.** The player needs MCD to research techs that unlock the machines that produce MCD components. The Fermentation Observation Log may be available early (from Era 2B fermenters), but Soil Microbiology Report, Colony Morphology Plate, and Microbial Population Count all require later techs. Either there must be a simpler bootstrap recipe for early MCD, or the first few techs accept Era 2 science packs (CED) instead.

**Assuming the bootstrap is handled** (e.g., initial Era 3 techs accept Era 2 CED, or a simplified "Preliminary Microbial Dataset" recipe exists using only fermentation products), the player progresses through the early animal and fermentation techs in hours 1-3.

### HOUR 3-6: The "8.2 Billion Per Gram" Reveal

- T-3.17 Soil Microbiology unlocks. The player builds the Soil Composition Analyzer and places it on a field plot they have been farming since Era 1. The readout appears: **"Microbial Population Density: 8.2 billion organisms per gram."** A notification: "Your decomposition vessels have been inoculating your field plots with beneficial microorganisms for [X] in-game hours. Soil fertility in composted plots is 34% higher."

**This is the strongest narrative moment in the first half of the game.** The player's Compost Heap from Era 1 was never breaking down waste. It was growing microbes.

- T-3.18 Microbial Isolation unlocks the Microbial Cultivator, Autoclave, Clean Bench. The player begins isolating pure cultures: Lactobacillus, Saccharomyces, Acetobacter. The fermentation chains now use identified cultures rather than wild fermentation.
- Dairy chain begins: Cow Milking Parlor -> Pasteurization -> Cream Separator -> Cheese Vat + Rennet + Lactobacillus -> Fresh Cheese Curd -> Aged Cheese Wheel. This is a multi-step chain but each machine runs passively.

**Emotional state:** Awe at the microbial reveal, then focused engagement setting up the dairy and fermentation infrastructure. Excitement: 8/10 at the reveal, settling to 6/10 during setup.

### HOUR 6-10: Fermenter Interference and the First "Aha"

- The player builds a fermentation wing: three Fermentation Vats in a row. Two use Lactobacillus (yogurt, vinegar), one uses Yeast (ethanol). The yeast fermenter underperforms. The player hovers and sees a **red down-arrow** (Era 3 hint arrows unlocked via T-3.27 Biological Web Awareness, ~hour 8). "Something is hurting my yeast." They move the yeast fermenter across the room. Output improves.

**This is the first deliberate "I need to plan my layout around biology" moment.** The fermenter competition relationship (-20% when yeast and lactobacillus are within 5 tiles) teaches that proximity is not always good.

- Power management becomes relevant. Era 3 total demand: ~410-585 kW. The player needs 4 Biogas Combustion Engines (4 x 120 kW = 480 kW) plus the Era 2 turbine (30 kW) = 510 kW. Biogas comes from the Anaerobic Digester processing crop waste and manure (T-3.16 unlocked around hour 5). **Power is intentionally tight in Era 3** -- the player must actively manage biogas production. This teaches fuel logistics when stakes are low.

### HOUR 10-14: Full Microbial Infrastructure

- Animal breeding pens set up. Dairy -> cheese aging cave (300s real-time for standard, 600s for premium). Aquaculture Pond (fish + algae commensal relationship).
- The Soil Composition Analyzer can now partially read crop Genetic Profiles: "Yield: Above Average, Growth Rate: Average, Hardiness: Below Average." Qualitative only, no numbers. But for the first time the player has DATA about hidden genetics.
- T-3.21 Symbiotic Inoculants unlocks: the player can deliberately manage soil microbes (Nitrogen-Fixing Inoculant, Mycorrhizal Inoculant). This is biological control of the Bio Web, not just passive observation.

### HOUR 14-18: Era Completion

- All 30 technologies researched. The player has established animal husbandry (cows, chickens, pigs, sheep, goats, silkworms, bees, fish), full fermentation infrastructure (6+ culture types), soil microbiology tools, and waste management (biofilter, vermicomposting, pyrolysis).
- The "phantom ingredient" fixes ensure no dead ends: Soybean substituted by Bean, Steamed Rice by Steamed Grain (Barley), Crystal Violet by Improvised Staining Dye, Rock Salt by Mineral Salt (water + ash from Era 1).

### TRANSITION: Era 3 to Era 4

- **Requirements met:** All 6 sections of tech tree completed (Y). Microbial cultures established (Y). Animal breeding active (Y).
- **Reward:** Access to "The Rules of Inheritance" chapter. The partial Genetic Profile data transitions to showing dominant/recessive traits.
- **Emotional payoff:** The player has gone from seeing organisms as commodities to understanding them as communities of invisible life.

### KEY METRICS AT ERA END:
- Items crafted: ~2000-3000
- Machines built: ~30 (18 main + 12 supporting)
- Science packs produced: ~6,000 MCD (30 techs x ~200 average)
- Power consumption: ~410-585 kW
- Bio Web entities: ~80-120
- Organism drift events observed: ~3-5 noticeable instances (yield variance in fermenters, milk output variation)
- Breadcrumbs found: Unidentified Microbial Culture (~5% from soil analysis), Anomalous Fermentation Byproduct (rare)

## ERA 4: THE RULES OF INHERITANCE (target: 16-20h)

### HOUR 1-6: Heredity Mapped

- The Heredity Analysis Station unlocks. For the first time, the player sees Punnett squares in the game UI. They breed two cows with known milk yield traits and predict offspring: "75% chance High yield, 25% chance Low." The offspring matches the prediction.

**This is the "biology is code" revelation.** The randomness the player observed in Era 3 breeding resolves into rules.

- Fractional Separation Column (distillation) and Molecular Assembly Workstation arrive. Chemistry serves biology: the player extracts alkaloids from plants, enzymes from fermentation, hormones from animal tissue.
- Genetic Profiles now show: "Yield: High (Dominant) / Low (Recessive), Growth Rate: Fast (Heterozygous)." Two traits remain locked ("Requires molecular analysis").
- Power: Biomass Steam Boiler (400 kW) from Compressed Biomass Pellets. One boiler + 2 carried Biogas Engines = 640 kW. Comfortable headroom.

### HOUR 6-12: The Ecology Analyzer

- **Major milestone:** The Ecology Analyzer tool unlocks. The player clicks their best wheat plot and sees for the first time: "Mutualistic with Legume-Clover (+15% yield). Commensal with Compost Bin (+10% yield). Competitive with Wheat Plot #7 (-8% yield)." **This is Revelation 3 from the signature mechanic spec: "The game has been doing this the whole time?"** The player re-evaluates every layout decision they have ever made.
- Drift history becomes visible: a simple graph of trait values over the last 10 drift events. The player notices organisms are changing. "My fermenter output has been slowly climbing."
- Stabilizer compound unlockable: reduces drift by 60% on target organisms. The player faces their first drift management decision: stabilize the high-performing wheat to lock in its good state, or let it keep climbing?

### HOUR 12-20: Breeding Programs and Chemical Tools

- Controlled breeding programs run autonomously once set up. The player selects trait targets and the Heredity Analysis Station predicts outcomes.
- Enzyme purification yields Catalytic Protein Isolates. Phytochemical Concentrates extracted from plants. These are ingredients for Era 5's molecular biology.
- Stabilized Cultivars produced: multi-generation crop lines with predictable trait expression. The player's "F7 Generation Select Wheat" consistently outperforms wild stock by 2-3x.

### TRANSITION: Era 4 to Era 5

- **Reward:** The notification reads: "If traits are carried by discrete units of biological information... what is that information made of?"
- **Emotional payoff:** The player understands heredity. They can predict offspring. The hook for Era 5 is irresistible: "I can read rules. Can I read the code?"

### KEY METRICS AT ERA END:
- Science packs produced: ~5,500 Hereditary Analysis Folios
- Power consumption: ~500 kW
- Bio Web entities: ~120-160 (+ breeding pens, analyzers)
- Organism drift events observed: Regular (every 5 minutes at era_scaling 1.0). Player actively monitors.
- Breadcrumbs found: Chimeric Seedling (~rare, from unusual cross)

## ERA 5: THE SOURCE CODE OF LIFE (target: 20-25h)

### HOUR 1-5: DNA Extraction and Sequencing

- Nucleotide chemistry chain established: Amino Acid Precursors (from Era 4) -> Nucleotide Synthesizer -> Adenine/Thymine/Guanine/Cytosine bases -> dNTPs -> Nucleotide Mix. This is a deep chain but each step is logical.
- Lysis Chamber -> Crude Genomic Lysate -> Silica Spin Column -> Purified Genomic DNA -> Fragmentation Sonicator -> Fragmented Library -> Genomic Sequencing Array. **The first full genome assembly takes ~20-30 minutes of real-time processing across the chain.**
- Power: Bio-Ethanol Fuel Cell Array (1.5 MW). The player needs 2-3 arrays (3.0-4.5 MW) to cover the CRISPR Lab (600 kW) + Sequencing Array (500 kW) + everything else.

### HOUR 5-8: The Genome Comparison -- THE Most Important Moment

- The player sequences their first organism: the same wheat cultivar they have been growing since Era 1. The genome data fills the screen. Then a comparison notification:

**"Genome comparison: Your current cultivar vs. archived wild variant from Era 1. 847 gene loci identified. 23 show significant divergence from wild type. All 23 divergences correspond to traits you selected for: yield, growth rate, hardiness."**

**"You did not know you were editing this genome. But you were. One harvest at a time, for [X] hours of gameplay, you have been rewriting this organism's source code through selection pressure."**

This is the emotional peak of the entire mod. Every hour of "farming" is retroactively revealed as genome editing at low resolution.

**Emotional state:** Awe. Genuine chills. Excitement: 10/10.

### HOUR 8-15: CRISPR -- The First Edit

- CRISPR Mechanism Discovery: the player isolates adaptive bacterial immunity from their own fermentation cultures. CRISPR is not "invented" -- it is found. Cas9 Protein extracted. Guide RNA designed from Gene Feature Annotations.
- **The first CRISPR edit chain:**
  1. Gene Feature Annotation (from genome analysis) -> Guide RNA Design Terminal -> Guide RNA Template
  2. Guide RNA Template + Cas9 Protein -> gRNA Assembly Chamber -> Assembled Guide RNA
  3. Assembled Guide RNA + Donor Template Cassette + Homology Arms + Selection Marker -> CRISPR Assembly Bench -> CRISPR Construct (Insertion)
  4. Target Organism + CRISPR Construct + Transfection Medium -> CRISPR Editing Lab (15s) -> Edited Genome (Unverified)
  5. Edited Genome + Off-Target Screening Report -> Verification Terminal (10s) -> Verified Edited Genome (92% success rate; 8% produces recoverable Partially Edited Genome)

**CHAIN DEPENDENCY CHECK: PASS.** Every ingredient is traceable to machines the player already has. The chain is deep (5 steps from annotation to verified edit) but each step takes 3-15 seconds. Total time for one edit: ~2-3 minutes including reagent prep. This is achievable without frustration.

- Web Overlay Toggle unlocked (Alt+B). Green and red lines appear between all related entities. The player's base is revealed as a web of interconnected life. This is visually stunning and information-dense.

### HOUR 15-25: Cloning, Cultured Meat, Biopharma

- Somatic Cell Nuclear Transfer: the player clones their best organisms. Clones drift independently.
- In Vitro Tissue Cultivation: Satellite Cell Isolate -> Myoblast Culture -> Cultured Myofiber Sheet -> Structured Meat Block -> Marbled Cultured Steak. First lab-grown food.
- Biopharma: Expression Constructs produce Recombinant Insulin, Growth Hormone, Interferon. High-value trade goods.
- Genome Export/Import: the player saves a wheat cultivar's trait vector to a Genome Export Chip. They can apply it to any wheat organism. "Golden seed" distribution across the farm.

### TRANSITION: Era 5 to Era 6

- **Reward:** Self-Modifying Bacterial Culture breadcrumb found. Anomalous Plasmid extracted. The Preliminary Genome Authoring Notes bridge to Era 6.
- **Emotional payoff:** The player has gone from reading genes to writing them. The question shifts from "can you?" to "should you?"

### KEY METRICS AT ERA END:
- Science packs produced: ~Genomic Annotation Compendium (sufficient for ~9 technologies)
- Power consumption: ~3-4 MW
- Bio Web entities: ~160-200 (+ CRISPR labs, cloning vats)
- Organism drift events observed: Constant (era_scaling 1.2). Drift is a background process the player monitors.
- Breadcrumbs found: Self-Modifying Bacterial Culture, Anomalous Plasmid

## ERA 6: AUTHORING LIFE (target: 20-25h)

### HOUR 1-8: Genome Authoring Platform

- Chassis Genome Preparation: stripped-down base genomes for synthetic organisms.
- Synthetic Gene Cassette Design: the player constructs multi-gene pathways (promoter + coding region + terminator).
- The Genome Authoring Platform unlocks: the player designs organisms from a design document, not from nature. Choose a chassis genome, insert pathways, set expression levels, synthesize.
- First Xenocrop: a grain plant that produces spider silk protein. The seed is synthesized, planted, grown. **"This organism was not found, bred, selected, or modified. It was authored."**
- Predictive Overlay unlocked: before placing, ghost-preview shows projected Bio Web relationships. The player can plan before committing.
- Genomic Lock compound: permanently freezes an organism's traits. The player locks their critical production organisms. The designed Era 6 wing is stable and engineered.

### HOUR 8-20: Designed Symbiont Pairs and Biopharma

- The player creates two Xenocrop organisms specifically designed to be mutualistic. Placed in alternating pattern: Alpha-Beta-Alpha-Beta. +30% yield bonus on all. This is the first time the player engineers the Bio Web directly.
- Pharmaceutical Pathway Integration: yeast engineered to produce insulin. Plants engineered to produce antimalarials.
- Meanwhile, the Era 3 fermentation wing (never locked) has been drifting for 40+ hours. The acetobacter culture has mutated. It now produces a novel compound. **The tension: control vs. emergence.**

### TRANSITION: Era 6 to Era 7

- **Power:** Enzymatic Bioreactor Turbine (5 MW) + carried Fuel Cell Array = 6.5 MW. Covers ~4-6 MW demand.
- **Reward:** Self-Editing Organism breadcrumb. An organism that rewrites its own genome in response to environmental stress.

## ERA 7: THE OPTIMIZATION ENGINE (target: 20-25h)

### HOUR 1-8: Directed Evolution

- Directed Evolution Chamber: set target trait, apply selection pressure, watch population converge over thousands of generations. 90s cycles (corrected from 45s). Drift rate at era_scaling 1.5 (fastest era).
- Strain Library: stores 50 organism variants, runs competitive screening, outputs highest-fitness variant.
- The player needs an algae strain producing 50% more biomass. They place algae next to a DEC, set selection pressure to "maximize yield_mod," apply mutagen. In 30 minutes of real time, yield_mod climbs from 1.03 to 1.41. They lock it. Clone it 12 times.

### HOUR 8-15: Industrial Biotech

- Algae farms, mycelium fabrication, bioplastics, biofuels. The player's biotech becomes infrastructure.
- Biofuel vehicles: Combine Harvester, Seeder, Sprayer -- all running on algal biodiesel.
- CO2 Exchange Mutualism: bioreactors pipe CO2 to photobioreactors, which return O2. +20% algae, +15% bioreactor efficiency. Industrial ecology.

### HOUR 15-25: Mk3 Upgrades and the Escaped Organism

- Mk3 modules for all previous machines: +100% speed, +50% yield. The player's entire production chain leaps forward.
- **Narrative event:** An organism escaped containment briefly. Soil samples show it survived, reproduced, and began interacting with native microbiome. "Interaction effects: undetermined." This is not a breadcrumb. This is a consequence.
- Power: Synthetic Metabolism Reactor (15 MW). Generous 50% surplus for many parallel DEC chambers.

### TRANSITION: Era 7 to Era 8

- **Reward:** Access to "Emergent Architecture."
- **Emotional payoff:** Evolution is powerful but not obedient. The organisms solve your problems -- and also create problems you did not ask about.

## ERA 8: EMERGENT ARCHITECTURE (target: 20-25h)

### HOUR 1-8: Living Systems

- Self-Healing Structural Biocomposite: walls interwoven with living mycelial networks. When damaged, the mycelium detects the breach and grows to fill it.
- Bioluminescent panels: engineered bioluminescent bacteria in biofilm matrix. Light output modulated by synthetic circadian circuit.
- Neural Mesh Node: +10% all stats, -50% drift rate within 12-tile radius. But the mesh node itself drifts.
- System Dashboard: map-level graph view of the entire Bio Web.

### HOUR 8-16: The First Living Building

- The player builds a production hall with living walls, bioluminescent lighting, mycelium floor, Neural Mesh Node at center. The overlay shows every entity in the room connected to the mesh (green). The system dashboard shows this room as a healthy green cluster.

## ERA 9: GENESIS (target: 30-40h)

### PHASE 0 -- BOOTSTRAP (Hours 1-5)

- The player researches T9-01 Living Architecture Theory (50 BGR after 75% cost reduction), T9-02 Totipotent Cell Engineering (38 BGR), T9-03 Morphogenetic Field Theory (38 BGR). Early techs unlock fast.
- BGR production begins: the Research Wing does not exist yet. **CRITICAL CHECK:** BGR is produced by the Research Wing, which is an Arcology module that requires a Living Architecture Seed to grow, which requires BGR to research. How does the player get the first BGR?

**Resolution:** T9-01 (Living Architecture Theory) is listed as costing 200 BGR (reduced to 50). But BGR comes from the Research Wing module, which requires T9-13 to build. This appears to be a sequencing problem.

**However**, looking at the tech tree prerequisites: T9-01 requires "All Era 8 techs complete." The cost is in Biosphere Genesis Records. But the document says in Part IX: "All Era 9 technologies require Biosphere Genesis Records plus all previous era science packs in declining quantities." And in Part VIII, Chain 4 shows BGR is produced BY the Research Wing.

**FINDING: POSSIBLE ERA 9 SCIENCE BOOTSTRAP ISSUE.** If BGR can only be produced by the Research Wing (an Arcology module), but the techs to build the Research Wing require BGR, there is a deadlock. The fix would likely be: (a) the first few Era 9 techs accept Era 8 science packs instead, or (b) a bootstrap BGR production facility exists pre-Arcology, similar to how Hemolymph/ATP have bootstrap machines.

**Assuming this is resolved** (e.g., Tier 1 Era 9 techs accept Era 7+8 science packs, BGR production only needed from Tier 2 onward), the player progresses through foundation techs and plants their first Living Architecture Seed.

- The seed grows for 48 minutes (34 with Growth Hormone Ampoule). During growth, the player designs the next module's layout on the Arcology Designer Table.

### PHASE 1 -- FIRST MODULES (Hours 5-15)

- **Energy Nexus built FIRST** (per bootstrap-machines.md recommendation). Once online: decommission all 3 ATP Catalytic Fermenters. ATP Solution now from Energy Nexus at 40/min (2x needed rate). Frees 1.5 MW.
- **Aquatic Systems built SECOND.** Once online: decommission all 4 Hemolymph Synthesis Vats. Hemolymph now passive at 60/min. Frees 1.6 MW.
- **Biodome built THIRD.** Primary crop growth, oxygen generation, photosynthesis.
- The first fluid loops close. The Arcology begins functioning. It is crude, it leaks, organisms drift. But it works.

**The decommissioning sequence is satisfying.** Each completed module lets the player tear down bootstrap machines. The base gets cleaner as the Arcology grows more complex.

### PHASE 2 -- FULL ORGANISM (Hours 15-25)

- All 9 modules come online: Biodome, Aquatic Systems, Livestock Haven, Energy Nexus, Kitchen & Provisioning, Workshop & Materials, Research Wing, Habitat, Ecology Monitor.
- The interaction matrix activates: +20% yield and +15% efficiency across ALL modules when all 9 are connected. The Arcology IS the Bio Web.
- The player installs the Homeostasis Engine and Biosphere Specification Console. For the first time, they are governing, not building. Setting mutation rate, drift tolerance, adaptive response parameters.
- The Synthetic Feast Platter is produced: molecular recreation of Era 1 bread and milk. The tooltip: "The bread is warm. The milk is cold. They taste exactly like those first days." **Breadcrumb payoff from the entire game.**

### PHASE 3 -- REFINEMENT (Hours 25-30)

- Fluid routing balanced. Organism populations tuned. Sustainability Score climbs past 80.
- Organism XR-00 (The Omega Organism) assembled: contains ALL genetic material from every organism the player ever created. Compilation takes 5 minutes. Assembly takes 10 minutes. "It watches you with eyes that contain the history of everything you built."
- The Remembrance Display crafted at the Arcology Designer Table -- the only Era 9 item the player makes with their own hands. Wild Wheat Specimen (from Era 1) + Complete Synthetic Genome + Ecosystem-in-a-Bottle + Pioneer's Journal. The display shows: left (wild wheat, unchanged), right (synthetic genome, everything you made), between them (a sealed bottle with something alive).

### PHASE 4 -- RELEASE (Hours 30-35)

- T9-22 Biosphere Self-Sufficiency researched. The 30-minute timer begins. All conditions must hold simultaneously:
  1. All 9 modules online and connected -- YES
  2. All fluid loops closed -- YES (12 fluids, all closed-loop)
  3. Energy balance positive -- YES (Mitochondrial Cluster + Photovoltaic Leaf Panels)
  4. Nutrient cycles complete -- YES (Lymphatic Filtrate recycled by Aquatic Systems)
  5. Sustainability Score >= 80 -- YES (tuned in Phase 3)
  6. Organism drift within tolerance -- YES (Homeostasis Engine managing)
  7. Habitat morale >= "Comfortable" -- YES (3+ food types, stable climate)
  8. No manual intervention required -- THE TEST

The player steps back. The Arcology breathes. For 30 minutes, nothing goes wrong. The Homeostasis Engine handles minor drift corrections automatically. The Ecology Monitor deploys Restoration Drones to a minor structural stress. The Aquatic Systems filters Lymphatic Filtrate on schedule.

At 30 minutes: "The Arcology has sustained itself for 30 minutes without intervention. It is alive."

Every era produces its first science pack within 30-60 minutes EXCEPT:
- **Era 3:** May have a 60-90 minute delay while bootstrapping the microbial analysis chain (soil analyzer, clean bench, staining station needed before full MCD recipe). Acceptable if early techs accept Era 2 science.
- **Era 9:** BGR production requires a functioning Research Wing module. The player cannot produce BGR until ~hour 10-12 of Era 9. This is by design (early techs may use Era 8 science packs).

Every era has verified power supply vs. demand with headroom. The tightest point is **Era 3** (510 kW supply vs. 410-585 kW demand), which is intentionally tight to teach fuel logistics.

- **First notice:** Era 2A hour 4-5 (Crop Vigor Pips)

Both are solvable with standard Factorio design patterns (earlier science packs accepted for entry techs).

## FINAL VERDICT

**Does the game work from start to finish?**

**YES -- with two caveats requiring minor fixes.**

The design is remarkably coherent across 165-215 hours. The emotional arc from wheat-picker to biosphere-author is the strongest narrative structure I have seen in a Factorio mod design. The six cross-era threads (Selection, Observation, Control, Scale, Uncertainty, Relationship to Life) each progress logically across all nine eras. The Bio Web and Organism Drift signature mechanics provide genuine emergent gameplay that deepens with each era. The breadcrumb system creates meaningful callbacks that reward early-game curiosity across the entire playthrough.

**Two issues requiring resolution before implementation:**

1. **Era 3 science pack bootstrap:** The Microbial Culture Dataset recipe requires components from machines that are gated behind technologies costing MCD. The first 2-3 Era 3 techs must either accept Era 2 CED as input, or a simplified "Preliminary Microbial Dataset" recipe must exist using only fermentation products available from Era 2B carryover.

2. **Era 9 BGR bootstrap:** Biosphere Genesis Records are produced only by the Research Wing (an Arcology module), but the techs to build Arcology modules require BGR. The Tier 1 Era 9 techs must accept Era 8 science packs, with BGR production becoming required only from Tier 2 onward (after the Research Wing can be constructed).

Neither issue is structural. Both are solvable with standard science-pack-bootstrapping patterns already used in Factorio modding.

**The game works.**
