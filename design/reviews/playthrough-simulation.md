# BIOGENESIS SEABLOCK — Simulated Playthrough Report

**Method:** Mental playthrough of all 9 eras from a first-time player's perspective, evaluating moment-to-moment experience, engagement curves, and design quality.

**Scoring:** Each era rated 1-10 on seven factors. 7 = solid, 8 = strong, 9 = exceptional, 10 = genre-defining.

---

## Scoring Key

| Factor | What It Measures |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Engagement** | How fun is the moment-to-moment gameplay? Would the player alt-tab? |
| **Pacing** | Are unlocks well-timed? Any dead spots or overwhelm? |
| **Theme** | Does it reinforce the biotech identity? Could this be ANY mod, or is it uniquely biological? |
| **Complexity** | Is the learning curve appropriate? Too simple = boring. Too complex = overwhelming. |
| **Satisfaction** | Does the era deliver on its promise? Does the marquee moment land? |
| **Factory Feel** | Does it feel like Factorio? Logistics puzzles, throughput optimization, belt spaghetti? |
| **Narrative** | Does the story/tone land? Do notifications and moments create emotional resonance? |

## ERA 1: Tidal Observations (1–1.5 hours)

**The Experience:** I spawn on a rock. Ocean everywhere. Seeds, mats, belts, a generator. Within 60 seconds I'm placing mats, planting seeds, laying belts. The 60-second crop cycle means I'm ALWAYS doing something — place mats while seeds grow, build machines while crops mature. Action-gated techs unlock every 8-10 minutes. At minute 30 I make STONE FROM SEAWATER and my brain lights up. By minute 90, Era 2.

**Marquee Moment:** Calcium Precipitate → Calcite. Stone from the ocean.

**Dead Spots:** None. Too short for dead spots.

| Factor | Score | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Engagement | **9** | New unlock every 8-10 min. Never idle. |
| Pacing | **10** | The tightest era. Perfect 90-min introduction. |
| Theme | **9** | Everything from seawater. Immediate seablock identity. |
| Complexity | **9** | 11 machines, ~50 items. Easy to learn, hints at depth. |
| Satisfaction | **9** | Stone from seawater is a genuine "holy shit" moment. |
| Factory Feel | **9** | Belts + inserters from minute one. It IS Factorio. |
| Narrative | **8** | Warm tone. "Kelp stalk. It will have to serve as timber." |
| **AVERAGE** | **9.0** | **A+** |

**Risk:** So good the player expects this pace to continue. Era 2's slower burn may feel jarring.

## ERA 2: Applied Cultivation (12–16 hours)

**The Experience:** Scale up, stockpile brackish water. Then IRON FROM BACTERIA. The trailer moment. Smelt it. Replace kelp belts with iron belts. Diatoms for glass. Copper from plants. GREEN CIRCUITS. Offshore platforms. Greenhouses. Seed classification.

**Marquee Moment:** Iron Precipitate → Bio-Smelter → Iron Plate. "I grew metal from seawater and bacteria."

**Dead Spots:** Hours 8-12. After copper/circuits, the remaining content (greenhouse, classification, bio-cement scaling) is necessary but not exciting.

| Factor | Score | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Engagement | **8** | Iron/diatoms/copper are exciting. Hours 8-12 dip. |
| Pacing | **7** | Front-loaded. Marquee moments packed in hours 2-8. Last 4 hours are scaling/setup. |
| Theme | **9** | "I grew metal from bacteria." Peak seablock. |
| Complexity | **8** | 15 machines. The interconnection diagram is essential. |
| Satisfaction | **9** | Iron from bacteria. Green circuits. Major milestones. |
| Factory Feel | **10** | Standard Factorio belts. Iron gears. Inserters. Peak factory. |
| Narrative | **8** | "Bacteria pulled this from the same seawater that tried to kill you." |
| **AVERAGE** | **8.4** | **A** |

**Recommendation:** Add a mid-era milestone at hours 8-10 (Bio Web bonus item trigger or archive notification) to break the scaling valley.

## ERA 3: The Invisible Ecosystem (12–16 hours)

**The Experience:** The Soil Analyzer hits like a truck. "47 identified strains. The bacteria you're using now are not the bacteria you started with." Then FIVE parallel systems dump on me: fermentation (7 outputs!), aquaculture (feed quality), mycelium, electrolysis, algae. Plus coral, sulfur, phosphorus, rubber. I'm pulled in every direction.

**Marquee Moment:** The Soil Composition Analyzer readout. "8.2 billion organisms per gram."

**Dead Spots:** Hours 10-14. Sulfur/phosphorus/advanced microbiology — complex, no single payoff.

| Factor | Score | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Engagement | **8** | Revelation is incredible. But 5 parallel systems dilute focus. |
| Pacing | **6** | OVERLOADED. 21 machines is nearly double Era 2. Too much per hour. |
| Theme | **10** | The BEST thematic era. Microbiology as core identity. |
| Complexity | **5** | TOO MUCH at once. The player needs a PhD to track it all. |
| Satisfaction | **8** | Analyzer is 10/10. Rest is "building systems I don't fully understand." |
| Factory Feel | **8** | Zoned districts look great. But complexity makes optimization hard. |
| Narrative | **9** | "8.2 billion organisms per gram. This soil didn't exist last week." |
| **AVERAGE** | **7.7** | **B+** |

**Recommendation:** Split into phases (3A: fermentation + aquaculture, hours 0-8; 3B: electrolysis + mycelium + advanced micro, hours 8-16). Gate 3B behind a mid-era milestone. Same content, better pacing. **This is the era most likely to make players quit.**

## ERA 4: The Rules of Inheritance (14–18 hours)

**The Experience:** Belt-based breeding. Load seeds, see Punnett square, design cross, incubate, trial, analyze, select, stabilize. First exclusive product is thrilling. Then repeat 7 more times. The PRODUCTS vary. The PROCESS doesn't.

**Marquee Moment:** Bio-Composite Plate. Steel equivalent from biology. "I never need to mine again."

**Dead Spots:** Hours 6-12. Breeding products 4-7. Same mechanic, different targets.

| Factor | Score | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Engagement | **7** | First 3 products: exciting. Next 5: routine. |
| Pacing | **7** | Front-loaded. Bio-composite at hour 6 is the peak. |
| Theme | **9** | Punnett squares make you feel like a geneticist. |
| Complexity | **7** | One system repeated. Not complex, just extensive. |
| Satisfaction | **8** | Bio-composite is major. Red circuits. Express logistics. |
| Factory Feel | **9** | Belt-based breeding line is brilliant Factorio design. |
| Narrative | **8** | "Offspring prediction: 75% high yield. Confirmed." |
| **AVERAGE** | **7.9** | **B+** |

**Recommendation:** Speed up breeding after 4+ products (2 generations to stabilize instead of 3). Make fish breeding mechanically different from plant breeding to add variety.

## ERA 5: The Source Code of Life (18–22 hours)

**The Experience:** CRISPR. 30-second edits replacing 15-minute breeding cycles. Sequencing my oldest crop: "23 diverged loci. You've been rewriting this genome since the first harvest." Bio-accumulation using the Era 1 kelp specimen — 60+ hours of archive storage paying off. Tissue culture. Living tools. Blue circuits from 4 eras of infrastructure. TRAINS. 1.5 MW fuel cell making ethanol supply critical.

**Marquee Moment:** The sequencing revelation. "You have been rewriting this genome since the first harvest."

**Dead Spots:** Few. Every 3-hour block has a marquee system.

| Factor | Score | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Engagement | **9** | CRISPR is a superpower. Bio-accumulation archive payoff. Trains. Multiple highs. |
| Pacing | **9** | New marquee system every 3 hours. Relentless. |
| Theme | **9** | "You can rewrite it. Should you?" Clinical gravity. |
| Complexity | **8** | CRISPR: easy to learn, hard to master (prioritization). |
| Satisfaction | **10** | Archive payoff. Blue circuits from 4 eras. Sequencing revelation. |
| Factory Feel | **9** | Trains connecting districts. Real throughput puzzles. |
| Narrative | **9** | 60+ hours of history in a genome readout. |
| **AVERAGE** | **9.0** | **A+** |

**Verdict:** Tied with Era 1 as the strongest era. CRISPR's power justifies Era 4's slower breeding — the contrast makes both better.

## ERA 6: Authoring Life (18–22 hours)

**The Experience:** Design organisms from scratch. Spider Silk Grain — an organism that doesn't exist, producing silk in its seeds. Phosphorescent moss spreading across the island. Methanotroph closing the biogas loop. Chimeras from fusing two organisms with unpredictable Bio Web interactions.

**Marquee Moment:** "Species: none. Kingdom: none. This genome was authored, not evolved. It belongs to you."

**Dead Spots:** Hours 12-15. Containment setup and pharmaceutical chain are necessary but not exciting.

| Factor | Score | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Engagement | **8** | Organism design is creative and satisfying. Chimera fusion adds variety. |
| Pacing | **8** | Good cadence. Fusion at hour 9 breaks pattern well. |
| Theme | **10** | Peak biotech fantasy. Designing life from scratch. |
| Complexity | **8** | Chassis + cassettes + promoters. Many recipes but linear pipeline. |
| Satisfaction | **9** | First xenocrop. Moss lighting the island. Methanotroph closing the loop. |
| Factory Feel | **7** | Spider silk line is factory. But organism DESIGN is more lab than factory. |
| Narrative | **9** | "It belongs to you." Weight and responsibility. |
| **AVERAGE** | **8.4** | **A** |

## ERA 7: The Optimization Engine (18–22 hours)

**The Experience:** Evolution Engine runs take 30 minutes. I build offshore infrastructure while waiting. Screening results: divergent strains are MORE interesting than targets. Cellulase transforms the ethanol economy. Bio-boats connect the archipelago. Then the containment breach choice: study vs contain.

**Marquee Moment:** The containment breach event. "The organisms here formed relationships your designed ecosystems never produced."

**Dead Spots:** The 30-minute waits between evolution runs if the player has finished parallel projects.

| Factor | Score | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Engagement | **7** | Divergent strains compelling. But 30-min waits between results. |
| Pacing | **7** | Long waits punctuated by screening bursts. Offshore fills time. |
| Theme | **9** | "Evolution doesn't follow your brief — it follows thermodynamics." |
| Complexity | **7** | Pipeline is straightforward. Strain catalog is the depth. |
| Satisfaction | **9** | Cellulase upgrading 4 eras of waste. Breach event choice. |
| Factory Feel | **7** | Engine is set-and-wait. Bio-boats recover factory feel. |
| Narrative | **10** | The breach event is the best single narrative beat in the game. |
| **AVERAGE** | **8.0** | **A-** |

**Recommendation:** Add "fast evolution" mode for re-runs of known targets (10 min vs 30). First run = discovery. Repeats = production.

## ERA 8: Emergent Architecture (18–22 hours)

**The Experience:** I grow walls. Neural mesh. Conduits pulse. Dashboard shows zone health. The wall resists demolition. I manage a living system's metabolism — nutrients, waste, connectivity. When health hits 120%, the building grows something I didn't design.

**Marquee Moment:** "The building is resisting demolition."

**Dead Spots:** Hours 12-15. Module construction is slow (1 hour per module).

| Factor | Score | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Engagement | **8** | Living architecture is novel. System health is a fresh mechanic. |
| Pacing | **7** | Growth timers create natural waits. Module construction is slow. |
| Theme | **10** | The factory IS an organism. Peak thematic delivery. |
| Complexity | **8** | System health is new but well-scaffolded by dashboard. |
| Satisfaction | **9** | Resistance moment. Self-organizing tissue mass breadcrumb. |
| Factory Feel | **6** | Ecosystem management, not factory building. Intentional shift. |
| Narrative | **10** | "The building is resisting demolition." Earned awe. |
| **AVERAGE** | **8.3** | **A** |

---

## ERA 9: Genesis (25–35 hours)

**The Experience:** 12 chimeras to commission (2-3 hours each). BEI climbing from 0 to 100 through active ecological tuning. Ecological events every 30-60 min keep me engaged. At BEI 90, the biosphere creates an organism I didn't design. The Omega Organism from 9 era specimens — 150 hours of archive storage. The camera pullback. The ending text.

**Marquee Moment:** "This is biogenesis. The origin of life. And you were it."

**Dead Spots:** Hours 8-25 during BEI tuning. Even with ecological events, the core loop is adjust-wait-observe between events.

| Factor | Score | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Engagement | **7** | Chimeras: engaging. BEI tuning: valleys between events. Omega: payoff of a lifetime. |
| Pacing | **6** | 25-35 hours is long. BEI tuning hours 8-25 is the highest slog risk. |
| Theme | **10** | "It doesn't need me." Transcendent. |
| Complexity | **8** | 12 chimera interactions + BEI + Omega. Complex but player has 8 eras of training. |
| Satisfaction | **10** | The ending. The archive payoff. BEI 100. Nothing in Factorio modding compares. |
| Factory Feel | **5** | Ecosystem management. Factory is background. Intentional. |
| Narrative | **10** | The best ending in any Factorio mod. |
| **AVERAGE** | **8.0** | **A-** |

**Recommendation:** Tighten to 20-28 hours. Make BEI tuning more impactful (+5 per successful intervention). The endgame should sprint, not marathon.

## TOP 5 RECOMMENDATIONS (priority order)

1. **Split Era 3 into 3A/3B phases** with a mid-era gate. The #1 risk for player dropout.
2. **Tighten Era 9 to 20-28 hours.** Make BEI tuning more impactful per intervention.
3. **Add fast-evolution mode in Era 7** for re-runs of known targets (10 min vs 30 min).
4. **Speed up late-era breeding in Era 4** after 4+ products (2 gen stabilization).
5. **Add mid-era milestone in Era 2** hours 8-10 to break the scaling valley.
