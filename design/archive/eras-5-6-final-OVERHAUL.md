## 8. ORGANISM DRIFT SPECIFICS

### Era 5 Parameters

- **era_scaling = 1.2** (slightly accelerated drift; organisms change faster than Era 4)
- **Rationale:** The player now has the tools to observe, measure, and counteract drift. Acceleration tests whether they use those tools.

### New Drift Tools

| Tool | Type | Effect | Duration/Cost |
|------|------|--------|---------------|
| CRISPR Drift Direction Cassette | Consumable, applied to organism | Steers the next 3 drift events toward a specified trait axis (e.g., "increase yield," "increase hardiness") | Consumed on application; lasts 3 drift events |
| Cloning Template (Organism) | Item, created from organism | Saves current genetic state; can be used to produce clones with identical genetics | 1x per organism snapshot; unlimited clones from template |
| Genome Export Chip | Item | Stores genome digitally; can be imported into a different organism of same species via Bioinformatics Terminal | Does not degrade; 1 genome per chip |
| Mutagenesis Compound | Consumable, applied to organism | +200% drift rate, +5% mutation chance for 10 drift events | Consumed on application |

### Drift Interaction with CRISPR

CRISPR edits modify the genome directly, but drift continues to operate on the modified genome. This means:
- An organism edited for high yield can still drift away from that trait.
- The player must either: re-edit periodically, clone from a stable template, or accept some variance.
- CRISPR Direction Cassettes provide a middle ground: temporary guidance without permanent lock.

### Drift and Cloning

Cloned organisms start with identical genetics to the source but immediately begin independent drift. Two clones from the same template will diverge over time. This is intentional and scientifically accurate.

### Drift and Mutagenesis

The Mutagenesis Compound is a high-risk, high-reward tool:
- Massively accelerates drift, useful for breeding programs or discovering new trait combinations.
- The +5% mutation chance means genuinely new traits can appear (traits not in the original species' trait pool).
- Combined with CRISPR Direction, this becomes "directed evolution in a bottle."

## 12. ANTI-GRIND MEASURES

### 12A. PCR Exponential Yield
PCR Amplicon crafts yield 8 units per cycle instead of 1. This reflects real PCR behavior (exponential amplification) and prevents the "I need 200 amplicons" tedium.

### 12B. Sequencing Parallelism
The Genomic Sequencing Array processes up to 4 fragmented libraries simultaneously. Throughput scales with the number of arrays. A single genome requires ~20 contigs, but with 2 arrays running, the player is never sequencing-bottlenecked.

### 12C. Bioinformatics Batching
The Bioinformatics Terminal accepts batch jobs: queue up to 10 annotation/assembly tasks. The player sets it and walks away. No babysitting.

### 12D. Cloning Eliminates Re-Breeding
Once a desirable organism is achieved, a single Cloning Template provides unlimited copies. The player never has to re-breed a lost lineage. The clone diverges via drift, but the template is permanent.

### 12E. Genome Export/Import Shortcuts
Genome Export Chips allow the player to transfer genetics between organisms of the same species without re-sequencing. Edit once, deploy many.

### 12F. Biosensor Automation
Biosensor Array Plates provide passive, ongoing environmental data. They connect to the circuit network, enabling automated responses (e.g., auto-fertilize when nutrients drop). The player builds the system once, then it self-manages.

### 12G. Cultured Meat Scales Linearly
Each Proliferation Bioreactor handles one cell line but outputs 4 culture flasks per cycle. Scaling cultured meat is a matter of adding bioreactors, not adding complexity.

### 12H. Reagent Mixer Bulk Mode
The Reagent Mixer accepts "bulk" recipes that produce 10x output for 8x input (20% efficiency bonus for batching). Available for Nucleotide Mix, PCR Master Mix, and all buffers.

### From Era 4 into Era 5
- **Amino Acid Precursors** (Era 4) are the primary feedstock for nucleotide synthesis.
- **Enzyme Purification Bench** (Era 4) produces Taq Polymerase, Cas9, and all molecular enzymes.

- **Cell Biopsy Station** (Era 4) is reused for somatic cell samples and satellite cell isolates.
- **Protein Expression Bioreactor** (Era 4) handles recombinant protein production.
- All Era 4 cultivars and organisms are the subjects of Era 5 genomic analysis. Nothing is discarded.

### From Era 5 into Era 6
- **Verified Edited Genomes** are the starting substrate for Era 6's Genome Authoring Platform.
- **Genome Export Chips** become the standard way to store and share organism designs.
- **Promoter Sequence Libraries** expand massively in Era 6 with synthetic promoters.
- **Cloning Templates** remain useful but are superseded by Era 6's Organism Designer.
- **Biosensor Array Plates** gain new probe types for detecting synthetic organisms.
- The **Self-Modifying Bacterial Culture** breadcrumb directly unlocks Era 6's keystone research.
- The **Anomalous Plasmid** is a physical ingredient in the Genome Authoring Platform recipe.

### Backward Connections (Era 5 improves earlier Eras)
- CRISPR edits can be applied to Era 2/3 crop varieties, retroactively improving them.
- Genome comparison works on any organism from any era, providing insight into all historical breeding.
- Biosensors improve Era 3/4 farming operations passively.
- Growth hormones (Auxin, Gibberellin) boost all plant growth rates from any era by 15%.
- Cultured meat replaces animal farming infrastructure from earlier eras entirely.

## 8. ORGANISM DRIFT SPECIFICS

### Era 6 Parameters

- **era_scaling = 1.0** (standard rate, returned from 1.2)
- **Rationale:** The player now possesses stabilization tools (Genomic Lock, Epigenetic Silencing, Designed Symbionts). Drift returns to standard rate because the player has earned control. The challenge shifts from "survive drift" to "design for stability."

### New Drift Tools

| Tool | Type | Effect | Duration/Cost |
|------|------|--------|---------------|
| Genomic Lock | Permanent modification | Drift rate = 0. Permanent. Organism never changes. | Expensive one-time cost (see recipe). Irreversible. |
| Organism Designer (trait specification) | Design tool | New organisms are created with player-specified traits. Drift starts from the designed state. | No ongoing cost; initial design cost. |
| Designed Symbiont Pairs | Pair deployment | Paired organisms stabilize each other. Drift rate reduced by 40% for both when paired. | Ongoing: symbiont pair must remain within range. |
| Epigenetic Silencing | Targeted modification | Specific traits locked via silencing. Those traits do not drift. Others still drift normally. | Moderate cost; can be reversed via Activation Construct. |
| CRISPRi/CRISPRa Toggle | Targeted modification | Reversible gene silencing/activation. Faster than full epigenetic modification. | Low cost; temporary (lasts 20 drift events). |

### Drift and Synthetic Organisms

Synthetic organisms drift like natural ones, but with these differences:
- Initial drift variance is lower (designed organisms start more stable).
- Drift direction is influenced by pathway validation (validated pathways drift less in the validated direction).
- Unvalidated pathways drift faster (penalty for skipping validation).

### Drift and Chimeras

Chimeric organisms have inherently higher drift (1.5x base rate) due to multi-kingdom gene instability. This is the price of chimeric power. Mitigation requires either:
- Genomic Lock (expensive, permanent)
- Constant epigenetic maintenance
- Designed containment (chimera containment fields reduce drift by 25%)

### Drift as Design Constraint

By Era 6, drift is no longer a problem to solve. It is a design parameter. The player decides:
- Which organisms to lock (expensive, permanent, zero flexibility)
- Which to allow to drift (cheap, flexible, requires monitoring)
- Which to stabilize partially (epigenetics, symbionts)
- The answer varies by use case. A production xenocrop should be locked. An experimental chimera should drift (to explore trait space). A symbiont pair should be partially stabilized.

## 12. ANTI-GRIND MEASURES

### 12A. Oligonucleotide Bulk Synthesis
The Oligonucleotide Synthesizer produces 10 oligos per craft. Since genes require 40-60, this means 4-6 crafts per gene, not 40-60. Reasonable.

### 12B. Gene Block Templates
Once a gene has been synthesized once, it is saved as a template. Subsequent copies require only nucleotides and 50% craft time. The player never redesigns the same gene.

### 12C. Pathway Blueprint Library
Validated pathways are stored permanently. The player can duplicate a validated pathway into new organisms without re-validating. Design once, deploy everywhere.

### 12D. Organism Designer Presets
The Organism Designer allows saving organism designs as templates. "Make another Arachniflora" is one click, not a 20-minute design session.

### 12E. Automated Containment Verification
The Containment Verification Report is automatically generated every 10 minutes for contained organisms, without player intervention. The player receives an alert only on failure.

### 12F. Xenocrop Auto-Harvest Integration
Xenocrops integrate with existing logistics (belts, inserters). Raw Spider-Silk Fiber, Crude Biocrude, Phosphor Sap, and Mineral-Enriched Tubers output to standard belt connections. No new logistics layer required.

### 12G. Parallel Genome Compilation
The Genome Compilation Engine accepts up to 3 genomes simultaneously. The player can boot multiple organism designs at once.

### 12H. Symbiont Pair One-Click Deployment
Paired Symbiont Units are placed as a single entity. The player does not manually place and align two organisms. One placement, one item, both symbionts deployed.

#### Fix UX3: CRISPR Failure Rate Reduction (from fixes-major-ux.md, Fix 3)

**Problem:** The Verified Edited Genome step in the CRISPR chain had an 80% success rate (20% failure). Each edit is 15+ steps deep. A failure destroyed all inputs and forced a full restart. The emotional cost was disproportionate.

**What changed:**

- **Base failure rate reduced from 20% to 8%.** Most edits now succeed.
- **Failure is now recoverable.** When the 8% failure occurs:
  - A new item **Partially Edited Genome** is produced (replaces total loss)
  - The Off-Target Screening Report IS consumed
  - All other inputs are RETURNED to the player
- **New recovery recipe:**
  ```
  Recipe: genome-edit-repair
    Inputs:  1 Partially Edited Genome + 1 Repair Enzyme Cocktail + 1 Fresh Off-Target Screening Report
    Machine: CRISPR Workstation
    Time:    20s (half of original edit time)
    Success: 95%
    Output:  1 Verified Edited Genome
  ```
- **New item: Repair Enzyme Cocktail**
  ```
  Recipe: repair-enzyme-cocktail
    Inputs:  1 Ligase Extract + 1 Polymerase Solution
    Machine: Enzyme Synthesis Station
    Time:    15s
    Output:  2 Repair Enzyme Cocktail
  ```

**Math:** Expected waste per genome edit: ~0.084 Screening Reports (vs old 0.20 × all inputs). Double-failure rate: 0.4%. Tension preserved but punishment massively reduced.

**New items:** Partially Edited Genome, Repair Enzyme Cocktail
