# PART 1: EXPANDED FLUID LIST

## Design Philosophy

Real agriculture is a wet industry. Every step involves water, juice, oil, slurry, wash, extract, fermentation broth, or waste effluent. The current 71 fluids underrepresent this reality, especially in the late game where biotech and industrial agriculture are even more fluid-intensive than traditional farming. The following additions bring the total to approximately 115 fluids.

The total of 133 overshoots the 100-120 target slightly. Candidates for cutting if needed (lower gameplay impact): flax wash water (Era 2), greenhouse condensate (Era 3), egg wash liquid (Era 4), smoke condensate (Era 6), syngas condensate (Era 8). Removing those 5 brings it to 128. Removing another 8-10 of the least mechanically interesting fluids would hit 120. However, I recommend keeping all 133 because fluids are cheap to define in Factorio modding (just a name, icon, color, and base temperature) and more fluids means more interesting pipe routing puzzles, which is core Factorio gameplay.

# PART 2: BIOTECH ENHANCEMENT PASS

## Design Philosophy

The progression should feel like the player is building a biotech company that happens to have started as a farm. By Era 9 the player should look at their base and see bioreactors, gene sequencers, and living architecture rather than barns and fields. Each era adds a biotech layer on top of the farming layer.

---

## ERA 5 — Biotech Foundations

**Theme:** The player discovers that biology can be an industrial process, not just food production. Enzymes, microbes, and biological systems start replacing chemical processes.

### New Machines (3)

- **Fungal Culture Chamber** — Grows filamentous fungi (Aspergillus, Trichoderma) on solid substrate. Requires temperature control (steam input). Outputs fungal biomass.
- **Enzyme Purification Column** — Takes fungal biomass or plant cell lysate, outputs purified enzyme preparations. Requires clean water input.
- **Bioremediation Pit** — Large 3x3 machine placed on polluted ground. Input: contaminated soil + water + microbial inoculant. Output over time: clean soil + bioremediation slurry. Slowly removes pollution in surrounding tiles.

### New Items (8)

| Item | Recipe Source | Key Ingredients | Use |
|------|-------------|-----------------|-----|
| **cellulase enzyme** | enzyme purification column | fungal biomass (Trichoderma) + fermentation broth | breaks down cellulose in plant waste; required for cellulosic processing in Era 8 |
| **protease enzyme** | enzyme purification column | fungal biomass (Aspergillus) + fermentation broth | meat tenderizing, leather softening, cheese making improvement |
| **lipase enzyme** | enzyme purification column | fungal biomass + plant oil substrate | fat processing efficiency (increases oil yield from oilseeds) |
| **amylase preparation** | enzyme purification column | fungal biomass (Aspergillus niger) + starch substrate | starch-to-sugar conversion; replaces malting in some recipes |
| **microbial inoculant** | fungal culture chamber | soil sample + nutrient solution + culture vessel | bioremediation pit input; also boosts crop yields when applied to soil |
| **bioremediation pack** | assembler | microbial inoculant + peat moss + activated carbon | portable soil decontamination (place on polluted tile) |
| **industrial citric acid** | industrial fermenter | sugar + Aspergillus niger culture + fermentation broth | food preservation, cleaning agent, pH adjuster for nutrient solutions |
| **bio-surfactant** | industrial fermenter | plant oil + Bacillus culture + fermentation broth | biodegradable cleaning agent; replaces chemical detergents |

### Key Recipes (7)

1. Fungal culture chamber: grain hulls + water + fungal spore packet = fungal biomass (slow, 60s)
2. Enzyme purification: fungal biomass + clean water = cellulase enzyme + spent culture medium
3. Enzyme purification: fungal biomass + clean water = protease enzyme + spent culture medium
4. Enzyme purification: fungal biomass + clean water = lipase enzyme + spent culture medium
5. Bioremediation pit: polluted soil + water + microbial inoculant = clean soil + bioremediation slurry (120s)
6. Industrial fermenter: sugar + Aspergillus culture = industrial citric acid + fermentation broth
7. Industrial fermenter: plant oil + Bacillus culture = bio-surfactant + fermentation broth

### New Fluids Introduced (from Part 1)
- enzyme solution, plant cell lysate, fermentation broth, bioremediation slurry

### Connections to Existing Systems
- Fungal culture chamber uses grain hulls (byproduct of Era 2 milling) and steam (Era 5 boiler)
- Enzyme purification uses clean water (Era 3 water treatment)
- Enzymes feed forward into Era 6 fermentation (amylase replaces malt in some recipes), Era 8 cellulosic ethanol (cellulase), and Era 4 cheese/leather processing (protease, lipase)
- Bioremediation provides the first active pollution cleanup mechanic, giving mid-game players a reason to invest in biotech rather than just ignoring pollution

## ERA 6 — Living Systems

**Theme:** The player moves from using dead biological products (enzymes, extracts) to managing living systems. Engineered yeasts, probiotic communities, and biofilm reactors are alive and must be maintained.

### New Machines (3)

- **Strain Engineering Bench** — Takes a base yeast/bacteria culture + selection medium + chemical mutagen or UV source. Outputs engineered strain with specific properties. Has a failure rate (sometimes produces dead culture).
- **Probiotic Incubator** — Maintains living microbial communities at controlled temperature. Input: starter culture + growth medium. Output: probiotic culture medium. Must be kept running continuously or cultures die.
- **Biofilm Reactor** — Attached-growth bioreactor where microbes grow on fixed surfaces. Continuous flow-through processing. Higher throughput than batch fermenters but requires biofilm maintenance (periodic cleaning cycle with lye solution).

### New Items (8)

|------|-------------|-----------------|-----|
| **engineered brewing yeast** | strain engineering bench | base yeast culture + selection medium + UV lamp | higher alcohol yield in fermentation; produces fewer off-flavors |
| **engineered chemical yeast** | strain engineering bench | base yeast culture + selection medium + mutagenic solution | produces industrial chemicals (succinic acid, butanol) instead of ethanol |
| **soil probiotic pack** | assembler | probiotic culture medium + peat carrier + packaging | apply to farmland for persistent yield bonus (lasts 10 crop cycles) |
| **animal probiotic feed** | feed mixer | probiotic culture medium + grain meal + molasses | animal health boost; reduces disease chance in livestock |
| **biofilm starter plate** | strain engineering bench | bacterial culture + agar plate + nutrient drops | seeds a biofilm reactor; must match reactor to desired output |
| **succinic acid** | biofilm reactor | engineered chemical yeast + sugar feedstock + CO2 | bioplastic precursor (feeds into Era 8), industrial chemical |
| **bio-butanol** | biofilm reactor | engineered chemical yeast + cellulosic hydrolysate | advanced biofuel (higher energy density than ethanol) |
| **koji starter culture** | strain engineering bench | Aspergillus oryzae sample + rice substrate + selection medium | required for koji fermentation (soy sauce, miso, sake) |

- Biofilm reactor uses sugar sources from Era 2-3 crops and cellulosic hydrolysate from Era 8 (forward dependency creates incentive to loop back)
- Probiotic systems connect to Era 3 soil science (soil amendments) and Era 4 animal husbandry (animal health)
- Succinic acid and lactic acid flow into Era 8 bioplastics

## ERA 7 — Full Biotech

**Theme:** This is the turning point. The player now has a genetics lab. CRISPR, synthetic biology, biopharma, and biosensors turn the farm into a biotech campus. The visual language should shift: clean rooms, PCR machines, gel electrophoresis rigs, growth chambers with indicator lights.

### New Machines (5)

- **CRISPR Workstation** — Precision gene editing. Input: target organism DNA + guide RNA + Cas9 protein + electroporation buffer. Output: edited organism with specific trait. Much higher success rate than mutagenic breeding. Requires clean room environment (must be placed inside enclosed lab room with air filtration).
- **Gene Synthesizer** — Builds DNA sequences from nucleotide stocks. Input: nucleotide stocks (A, T, G, C chemicals) + synthetic gene template (from research). Output: synthetic gene solution. Expensive but allows designing traits that do not exist in nature.
- **Biopharma Fermentation Vessel** — Large 3x3 vessel. Grows engineered organisms that produce pharmaceutical compounds. Very slow (300s per batch). Output: crude pharmaceutical extract.
- **Biosensor Fabrication Unit** — Assembles living biosensor modules: organisms engineered to change color or produce signal molecules in response to environmental conditions. Output: biosensor probes.
- **Microbial Fuel Cell** — Generates electricity from organic waste using electroactive bacteria. Input: organic waste + anaerobic bacteria culture. Output: small continuous power + spent culture medium. Not high power, but runs on waste.

### New Items (10)

| Item | Recipe Source | Key Ingredients | Use |
|------|-------------|-----------------|-----|
| **Cas9 protein** | enzyme purification column | engineered E. coli culture + purification reagents | CRISPR gene editing (the molecular scissors) |
| **guide RNA template** | gene synthesizer | nucleotide stocks + target gene sequence data | directs CRISPR to correct location; specific per trait |
| **CRISPR-edited seed** | CRISPR workstation | crop seed + Cas9 + guide RNA + electroporation buffer | crop with precision trait (drought resistance, yield, nutrition) |
| **CRISPR-edited embryo** | CRISPR workstation | livestock embryo + Cas9 + guide RNA + embryo transfer medium | livestock with precision trait (disease resistance, growth rate) |
| **synthetic gene construct** | gene synthesizer | nucleotide stocks (A/T/G/C) + gene template | novel genes not found in nature; input for synthetic organisms |
| **synthetic organism culture** | biopharma vessel | synthetic gene construct + host cell line + tissue culture medium | organism that produces target compound by design |
| **crude pharmaceutical extract** | biopharma vessel | engineered plant/animal tissue + extraction solvent | refined into specific pharmaceuticals |
| **recombinant insulin** | chemical processing | crude pharmaceutical extract + purification column processing | high-value trade good; medical supply |
| **recombinant growth hormone** | chemical processing | crude pharmaceutical extract + purification column processing | accelerates livestock growth when added to feed |
| **biosensor probe** | biosensor fabrication unit | engineered bioluminescent bacteria + sensor housing + nutrient pack | place in field/water; changes signal color based on N/P/pH/disease; provides data to smart farming system |

### Key Recipes (10)

1. Enzyme purification: engineered E. coli biomass + PCR buffer solution = Cas9 protein + spent culture medium
2. Gene synthesizer: nucleotide A + T + G + C stocks + gene template = guide RNA template (per trait type; 30s)
3. Gene synthesizer: nucleotide stocks + synthetic gene template = synthetic gene construct (60s)
4. CRISPR workstation: crop seed + Cas9 protein + guide RNA + electroporation buffer = CRISPR-edited seed (45s, 85% success)
5. CRISPR workstation: livestock embryo + Cas9 protein + guide RNA + embryo transfer medium = CRISPR-edited embryo (90s, 70% success)
6. Biopharma vessel: synthetic organism culture + tissue culture medium + oxygen = crude pharmaceutical extract (300s)
7. Chemical processing: crude pharmaceutical extract + ethanol + purification reagents = recombinant insulin (120s)
8. Chemical processing: crude pharmaceutical extract + purification reagents = recombinant growth hormone (120s)
9. Biosensor fabrication: engineered bioluminescent bacteria + plastic housing + nutrient gel pack = biosensor probe (30s)
10. Microbial fuel cell: organic waste slurry + electroactive bacteria = 200kW continuous power + spent culture medium

### New Fluids Introduced (from Part 1)
- tissue culture medium, PCR buffer solution, electroporation buffer, restriction enzyme cocktail, embryo transfer medium, cryoprotectant solution, protoplast suspension, selection medium, gibberellin solution, Agrobacterium suspension, spent culture medium, synthetic gene solution

### Connections to Existing Systems
- CRISPR workstation replaces/upgrades the existing mutagenic breeding system (Era 7 already has mutagenic solution). CRISPR is more precise: mutagenic breeding has random trait outcomes, CRISPR targets specific traits. Player should transition from mutagenic to CRISPR.
- Existing auxin and cytokinin fluids are used in tissue culture medium, connecting the current Era 7 hormones to the new biotech pipeline.
- Biopharma connects to Era 4 animal husbandry (growth hormone for livestock), Era 5 chemistry (purification steps), and creates high-value trade goods.
- Biosensors connect to Era 3 soil science (monitoring) and provide data bonuses that increase efficiency of all farming operations in range.
- Microbial fuel cells use organic waste from Era 4 livestock and Era 6 fermentation byproducts, turning waste streams into power.
- Nucleotide stocks (A, T, G, C) are a new raw material produced by chemical processing from existing Era 5 chemicals. This creates a supply chain bottleneck that makes gene editing feel expensive and earned.

## ERA 8 — Industrial Biotech

**Theme:** Everything that was petroleum-derived gets a biological replacement. The player is now running an industrial biotech operation: bioplastics, mycelium composites, algae farms, and bio-based chemical plants. Scale is the challenge.

### New Machines (4)

- **Algae Photobioreactor** — Large transparent reactor (illuminated, visually distinct). Input: algae culture medium + CO2-enriched air + light. Output: algae biomass at high rate. The workhorse of Era 8. Must be placed where it receives light (outdoor or under artificial lighting).
- **Bioplastic Injection Molder** — Takes polylactic acid melt or other biopolymer fluids, molds them into products. Replaces petroleum plastic in all recipes that require plastic.
- **Mycelium Growth Chamber** — Grows mycelium through substrate in molds. Input: mycelium growth medium + sawdust/agricultural waste + mold form. Output: mycelium composite block (after 120s cure time). Structural material.
- **Biomass Pyrolysis Unit** — Thermal decomposition of biomass in oxygen-free environment. Input: woody biomass + heat. Output: pyrolysis oil + biochar + syngas. More efficient than basic biomass burning.

| **bioplastic sheet** | bioplastic injection molder | polylactic acid melt | replaces petroleum plastic in packaging, containers |

### Connections to Existing Systems
- Algae photobioreactor uses CO2 from Era 6 fermentation exhaust (beer/wine production) or Era 8 combustion, creating a carbon loop
- Cellulase enzyme from Era 5 fungal cultures is required for cellulosic hydrolysate, making Era 5 biotech mandatory infrastructure
- Engineered yeast from Era 6 strain engineering bench is required for cellulosic ethanol, ensuring the living systems pipeline feeds forward
- Lactic acid from Era 6 fermentation becomes PLA bioplastic, so the fermentation investment pays off industrially
- Mycelium composites use sawdust and agricultural waste (crop stover, straw) from Era 2-3 farming, giving value to previously low-value byproducts
- Biochar feeds back into Era 3 soil science as a permanent soil amendment
- Algae protein powder competes with traditional animal feed, giving the player a choice between livestock-heavy and algae-heavy protein production paths
- Existing biodiesel recipe (Era 8) gains algae oil as an alternative feedstock to plant oils, massively increasing throughput potential

## ERA 9 — Arcology Biotech

**Theme:** The endgame. The player builds a closed-loop biological arcology where every waste is a resource, buildings heal themselves, lights are alive, and the atmosphere is regulated by engineered organisms. The farm has become a living city.

### New Machines (5)

- **Atmospheric Bioprocessor** — Large structure containing engineered photosynthetic organisms. Input: CO2-enriched air + light + nutrient broth. Output: oxygen-enriched air + biomass. Maintains breathable atmosphere in sealed arcology.
- **MICP Reactor (Microbially-Induced Calcite Precipitation)** — Bacteria precipitate calcium carbonate, creating bio-cement. Input: ureolytic bacteria culture + urea + calcium source + water. Output: bio-cement slurry. Slow but self-repairing.
- **Bioluminescence Culture Vat** — Grows bioluminescent organisms (dinoflagellates or engineered bacteria). Input: culture medium + nutrients. Output: bioluminescent extract. Continuous process, visually glowing.
- **Universal Nutrient Synthesizer** — Endgame machine. Takes elemental inputs (minerals, trace elements, water, nitrogen) and synthesizes any nutrient solution. Replaces dozens of individual nutrient mixing recipes with one flexible machine.
- **Closed-Loop Life Support Module** — The capstone machine. Integrates water recycling, air processing, waste treatment, and nutrient recovery into one large (4x4) structure. Represents the fully functional arcology life support system.

## CROSS-ERA DEPENDENCY CHAINS

The biotech additions create several long supply chains that reward players who invest across multiple eras. Key chains:

**The Bioplastic Chain (Eras 2-3-5-6-8):**
Crops (Era 2) --> sugar extraction (Era 3) --> fermentation (Era 5-6) --> lactic acid (Era 6) --> polymerization (Era 8) --> bioplastic products (Era 8)

**The Gene Editing Chain (Eras 4-5-6-7):**
Livestock/crops (Era 4) --> enzyme engineering (Era 5) --> strain engineering (Era 6) --> CRISPR workstation (Era 7) --> edited organisms (Era 7+)

**The Cellulosic Ethanol Chain (Eras 2-5-6-8):**
Crop stover waste (Era 2) --> cellulase enzyme (Era 5) --> enzymatic hydrolysis (Era 8) --> engineered yeast fermentation (Era 6 yeast, Era 8 reactor) --> cellulosic ethanol (Era 8)

**The Closed-Loop Chain (Eras 3-5-7-8-9):**
Soil science (Era 3) --> bioremediation (Era 5) --> biosensors (Era 7) --> algae/atmospheric processing (Era 8) --> full life support (Era 9)

**The Biopharma Chain (Eras 5-6-7):**
Fungal cultures (Era 5) --> Cas9 protein (Era 5 enzyme purification) --> CRISPR editing (Era 7) --> synthetic organisms (Era 7) --> pharmaceutical production (Era 7)

These chains ensure that biotech is not a standalone system but deeply integrated with all previous farming and processing infrastructure. The player cannot skip to the endgame without building the foundation.
